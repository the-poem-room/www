Pendulum

1 At thirteen, I thought desire meant doing. 2 That love had to move forward, 3 that progress meant skin, 4 not understanding.

5 I learned to measure myself 6 by how much someone else 7 wanted to touch me, 8 and I mistook that for meaning.

9 Each girl became a mirror 10 tilted at a different angle, 11 and I kept asking my reflection 12 to look more like a man.

13 Somewhere in these years 14 a flower grew in me— 15 soft, uncertain, too bright. 16 I kept trimming it back, 17 afraid it made me less.

18 I chased comfort like a currency, 19 collected validation like breath, 20 and built entire futures 21 on the phrase I love you, 22 because it made me real.

23 When they turned away, 24 the silence felt like proof 25 that I wasn't enough 26 that warmth was something 27 only given, never grown.

28 Then someone came along 29 whose mind lit up like mine: 30 quick, curious, kind, 31 but her eyes searched for a man 32 I could only perform, never become. 33 Her wanting bruised me softly. 34 I admired her brilliance, 35 but not her hunger 36 for the shape she thought I was.

37 Later, there was someone beautiful, 38 beautiful like the clarity, 39 beautiful like the first thing you notice 40 and the last thing you understand. 41 But we kept breaking, 42 reassembling in slightly wrong shapes, 43 calling it love, 44 calling it trying.

45 I stopped needing her to hold me 46 to prove I existed. 47 Stopped needing to be the hboy 48 she could fix, or tear, or forgive. 49 I simply stopped, 50 and that was its own freedom.

51 Now, I find myself drawn 52 to softness unclaimed by roles, 53 to people who don't gender their gentleness, 54 who find beauty in divergence 55 and speak in frequencies 56 I don't have to translate.

57 That old flower still grows— 58 once soft and shameful, 59 now honest and unlabelled. 60 It leans toward its own light, 61 petals shifting, undefined, 62 become whatever warmth allows.

63 The pendulum slows. 64 I'm still learning 65 what movement means 66 when it isn't toward someone. 67 I don't know what I'll seek next— 68 whose laughter will stay, 69 whose eyes I'll meet 70 without pretending first.

71 But the world feels wider now, 72 like a garden waking in me, 73 and I've only just begun 74 to name the petals.

— Lilith
