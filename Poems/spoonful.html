Spoonful

Jump to stanza

1 This morning 2 I tipped a spoon of chia seeds 3 onto my Weetabix— 4 the same small ritual 5 I barely notice anymore.

6 Later, I looked into the tub 7 and found the bottom.

8 It startled me, 9 how something so tiny, 10 so ordinary, 11 could empty a whole container 12 without ever announcing itself, 13 without ever realising 14 what it had become.

15 I tried to remember 16 the moment I refilled it— 17 it feels impossibly recent, 18 as if time had cheated, 19 as if progress had happened 20 quietly, 21 behind my back.

22 One spoonful. 23 Then another. 24 Then another.

25 No single morning 26 felt like change. 27 But together 28 they became disappearance. 29 Proof that repetition 30 moves without noise 31 not just in kitchens, 32 but everywhere.

33 That small 34 keeps going 35 until it is no longer small.

36 I thought of hands 37 that signed the same petition 38 in cold halls and crowded streets 39 again and again 40 until signatures turned into law.

41 Of voices 42 that kept speaking 43 long after they were told 44 to be silent, 45 until silence itself 46 was forced to move.

47 Of feet 48 that walked the same streets 49 in the same direction 50 for years, 51 until roads learned 52 new meanings; 53 until history found 54 new routes.

55 Of people 56 who did not overthrow the world 57 in one dramatic moment, 58 but nudged it, 59 daily, stubbornly, 60 spoonful by spoonful, 61 day by day.

62 It made me strangely hopeful: 63 that the things I do 64 when nobody is watching, 65 when nothing feels different, 66 when change seems too slow to matter, 67 might already be emptying a tub 68 I haven't thought to look inside, 69 in lives I will never see.

70 That maybe 71 the future is not built 72 by revolutions alone, 73 but by breakfasts, 74 by habits, 75 by ordinary mornings 76 that refuse to give up, 77 even when they feel 78 too small to matter.

79 And tomorrow, 80 I will sprinkle another spoonful 81 without expecting anything. 82 But the tub will be closer to empty. 83 And the world— 84 just slightly, 85 almost invisibly, 86 closer to becoming 87 something kinder 88 than it ever was.

— Lilith
