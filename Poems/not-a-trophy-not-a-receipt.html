Not a Trophy, Not a Receipt

64 lines · 339 words · 4 min read

1 Who taught the corridor 2 to rank us like league tables— 3 as if a girl beside you 4 adds points to your predicted grade?

5 Gross.

6 Who priced a smile in clout, 7 turned hand-holding into a logo, 8 stamped "exclusive" on a person 9 like a limited-run trainer drop?

10 Gross.

11 I'm seventeen and tired of it: 12 the whisper that a date is currency, 13 the boys are banks, girls are gold, 14 and status is the interest we collect.

15 No.

16 She isn't a badge to pin on a blazer, 17 I'm not a cabinet with glass doors. 18 We are not unlockable achievements, 19 season-pass skins for public approval.

20 Love isn't a CV booster. 21 You can't "earn" a person 22 with grind, charm, or cheat codes. 23 No one is a prise for finishing the level.

24 Stop calling them "out of your league." 25 We're not fixtures. We're not scores. 26 We're people with headaches, deadlines, 27 bad jokes, soft fears, and loud laughs.

28 If your mates clap louder 29 when she says yes 30 than when she says what she wants— 31 something's rotten in the rules.

32 If an app tells her she's valuable 33 when she's chosen, posted, claimed, 34 and silent otherwise— 35 smash the metric, not her voice.

36 I refuse the trade. 37 No auctions in my mouth, 38 no receipts in my pocket, 39 no trophies on my arm.

40 Here's my counterspell: 41 One person, whole and happy— 42 not a half hunting a half; 43 or two, if chosen; or none for now— 44 a life that nobody scores.

45 Monogamy, hetero, queer, poly, solo— 46 none of them a ladder to the "good life," 47 just routes on a map you can skip, 48 detours I'm free not to take.

49 I'm often most alive when single, 50 unclaimed, unbranded, unmeasured— 51 tim to hear my own music 52 without the crowd yelling "pair up."

53 Call it radical, or just normal. 54 Call it respect with the volume on. 55 Call it what should have been 56 before the marketplace moved in.

57 And if the crowd still chants 58 win, win, win— 59 I'll answer: 60 We're not playing your game. 61 We're writing our own— 62 and mine doesn't require a plus-one.

63 Everything else? 64 Gross.

— Lilith
