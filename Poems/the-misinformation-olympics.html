The Misinformation Olympics

84 lines · 311 words · 4 min read

1 I'm told 2 I'll fade without meat — 3 usually by someone halfway through a drive-thru, 4 confidence dripping with grease, 5 arteries rehearing their last act.

6 You're not getting enough protein. 7 You're iron-deficient. 8 You need B12. 9 That vegan food's too artificial.

10 Artificial. 11 That word lands heavy 12 from people whose meals 13 were factory-made 14 before the cow was born.

15 They bite into burgers 16 with twelve grams of protein 17 and twice the saturated fat, 18 then lecture me 19 while I've had nineteen before noon — 20 from pumpkin seeds, not pork — 21 and tofu waiting 22 like quiet triumph 23 in the fridge for later.

24 It's a not a competition, 25 but if it were, 26 I'd win. 27 Mine's the only diet 28 with maths, not myths; 29 with fibre that feeds the gut, 30 not grease that feeds disease.

31 They call my food fake, 32 but theirs is built on needles and nerves — 33 animals swollen with antibiotics, 34 force-fed soy, 35 their flesh marbled 36 with the same saturated fat 37 that builds walls 38 around the human heart.

39 They call my food unnatural 40 while their sausages 41 are stitched from scraps and salt, 42 their eggs from hens 43 who never saw daylight, 44 their plates loaded 45 with sodium and denial.

46 I eat colour, 47 fibre, 48 sunlight pressed into leaves. 49 They eat nostalgia — 50 battered, fried, 51 and seasoned with excuses.

52 Yet somehow, I'm the one 53 who's "missing out."

54 No. 55 Here's the truth: 56 I'm not undernourished. 57 I'm just inconvenient.

58 My existence 59 interrupts the story 60 they tell themselves 61 to sleep better.

62 Because if I can live 63 stronger, cleaner, kinder 64 without killing, 65 then maybe their comfort food 66 starts to taste 67 a little guilty.

68 That's what really bothers them. 69 Not my protein. 70 Not my iron. 71 Not my B12.

72 Just the quiet, undeniable proof 73 that compassion 74 and health 75 can share a body.

76 So yes — 77 I'll keep eating my pumpkin seeds. 78 My tofu. My beans. My conscience.

79 You keep your comfort food, 80 your myths, 81 your artificial innocence.

82 We'll see 83 who survives 84 the syllabus of science.

— Lilith
