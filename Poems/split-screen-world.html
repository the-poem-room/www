Split-Screen World

38 lines · 178 words · 2 min read

Jump to stanza

1 Two rectangles of glass, 2 stacked one on top of the other— 3 your half and mine. 4 The TV hums like a campfire, 5 casting mountain light across our living room. 6 We built our home into the cliffs, 7 windows cut in stone 8 like watchful eyes 9 over the blocky valleys. 10 A cobblestone staircase spiralled 11 down to the little town 12 we made from nothing— 13 oak doors creaking 14 as we pushed them open, 15 torches swaying in their pixel wind. 16 Wheat grew slow in our square plots, 17 rows neat under our care, 18 no redstone, no automation— 19 just the rhythm of hands, 20 hoe striking dirt, 21 seeds placed one by one. 22 I can still see your smile 23 when I baked you a cake— 24 our own small joke, 25 funnier each time— 26 still hear rain patter 27 on our spruce-slab roof, 28 still feel the weight of the controller 29 and the quiet comfort of knowing 30 that whatever the Creepers took, 31 we would rebuild. 32 Somewhere, 33 on a save file sleeping in a dusty hard drive, 34 our mountain house waits— 35 torches still burning, 36 wheat still swaying, 37 our blocky selves 38 forever side by side. — Lilith
