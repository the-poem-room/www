Softness Was Always His

Jump to stanza

1 "Dudes can wear dresses if they're gay," 2 says the joke, tossed lightly 3 across a room of laughter, 4 stitched with threads of satire. 5 It lands with a spark 6 not to burn, but to show 7 how thin the fabric is 8 between cloth and meaning.

9 A dress is only fabric. 10 Buttons, seams, breath. 11 Yet it carries 12 the weight of centuries 13 of "should" and "shouldn't."

14 We laugh because it's absurd, 15 but the absurdity isn't the dress. 16 It's the rule that insists 17 fabric has a gender, 18 that silk has a sexuality, 19 that cloth must confess.

20 Maybe the joke isn't about dresses. 21 Maybe it's about 22 how small we made the world 23 just to feel certain inside it.

24 And somewhere, between the punchline 25 and the quiet after, 26 a man reaches for softness 27 not because he is "allowed," 28 but because softness was always his.

— Lilith
