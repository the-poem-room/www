Permutations

What's the point?

Permutations.

Permutations
of silence.

Permutations
of wanting.

Permutations
of bodies
beside bodies.

Permutations
of I'm fine
rearranged
until believable.

Permutations
of grief.

Permutations
of hunger.

Permutations
of names
inside old group chats.

Permutations
of almost.

Permutations
of touch.

Permutations
of history
misremembering itself.

Permutations
of children
becoming parents
becoming photographs.

Permutations
of God.

Permutations
of loneliness
wearing different coats.

Permutations
of stars,
of dust,
of teeth,
of traffic lights,
of apologies typed
then deleted.

Permutations
of stay.

Permutations
of go.

Permutations
of meaning.

Permutations
of the question
repeating itself
through different mouths.

What's the point?

Permutations.

— Lilith
