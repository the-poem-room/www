The Real Divide

33 lines · 151 words · 2 min read

1 They keep calling it left and right, 2 as if truth wears a colour, 3 as if a fact can wave a flag.

4 But it isn’t that. 5 It’s who builds their world 6     on something reasoned, 7         and who builds it on noise.

8 It’s not a party line— 9 it’s a lifeline. 10 A test of who can bear 11     the weight of being wrong— 12         not the kind you lift in a gym, 13             but the kind that makes thought ache.

14 We’re fighting the wrong war, 15     cheering for teams 16         while the scoreboard burns.

17 Some call it common sense, 18     mistaking comfort 19         for clarity. 20 Some shout facts don’t care about feelings, 21     then sob 22         when theirs are scratched.

23 It’s not blue or red anymore— 24     it’s evidence, 25         or echo. 26 Reason, 27     or rumour. 28 A map drawn from data, 29     or from desire.

30 And I don’t care what side you’re on, 31     if you can still look a fact in the eye 32         and say, 33             I believe you.

— Lilith
