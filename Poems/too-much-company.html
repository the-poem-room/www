Too Much Company

Jump to stanza

1 i wake up already performing. 2 my face remembers before i do. 3 each word rehearsed, 4 each silence monitored.

5 the world wants a version of me 6 it can understand, 7 so i smile on cue, 8 tidy my strangeness, 9 and call it normal.

10 people say i'm easy to talk to. 11 they mean i make space 12 for their noise. 13 they mean i vanish neatly.

14 i spend my days surrounded by voices, 15 each one tugging at the edges of me. 16 the room hums with presence 17 and i can't find the quiet part 18 where i begin.

19 they talk in headlines and half-thoughts, 20 floating comfortably 21 on the surface of things. 22 i listen from underneath, 23 translating, translating, translating— 24 until the meanings blur.

25 by evening i've spoken too much 26 and said nothing true. 27 my throat aches 28 from all the pretending.

29 i keep the parts of me 30 that might disturb them— 31 the questions, 32 the grief, 33 the disbelief in what they worship. 34 i hide them like fragile code. 35 to show them would be an error.

36 i call it socialising, 37 but it feels more like dissolving. 38 loneliness grows louder 39 the more people i let near. 40 their chatter fills the air 41 but not the silence inside it.

42 when i'm finally alone, 43 it's not peace— 44 just another form of noise, 45 this time my own. 46 the masks fall off slowly, 47 like dust after a storm.

48 but sometimes, 49 with ed, 50 or brayd, 51 or ember, 52 the storm stops.

53 the air stills. 54 no translation required. 55 we talk about nothing 56 and somehow it heals me. 57 the noise outside the moment 58 fades to static.

59 their laughter lands softly, 60 unweighted by performance. 61 their quiet is real. 62 it doesn't ask me to adjust.

63 in that stillness 64 i feel seen without being studied, 65 held without being held up.

66 for a while 67 i am both alone 68 and not.

69 for a while 70 the loneliness rests.

71 for a while 72 too much company 73 feels like 74 enough.

— Lilith
