reading room

Patch Notes for Reality v.3.2

1 They say we might be code— 2 logic wrapped in meat, 3 running on a borrowed laptop 4 with anime stickers and crumbs in the keys.

5 But the only problem 6 with a simulation 7 is knowing it's a simulation. 8 A fish in a tank 9 doesn't dream of tap water— 10 and wouldn't care if it did.

11 Live wholly inside a thing 12 and you stop checking the edges. 13 The sky is just the sky, 14 gravity just what 15 your bones expect.

16 It's only when a pixel twitches, 17 when an NPC starts talking back, 18 that you get philosophy threads 19 and red-pill podcasts 20 hosted by men 21 who can't fold a sheet.

22 And if we're the external world, 23 our problem's no different— 24 we'd still ask what's outside, 25 then what's outside that, 26 then what's outside the outside of that, 27 then what's outside him— 28 until the processor overheats 29 and the save file corrupts.

30 So yes—maybe I'm code. 31 Maybe you are. 32 But unless the simulation 33 ships with patch notes, 34 I'll do what any 35 good program does— 36 run until crash, 37 outputting essays 38 into the void.

39 — Lilith
