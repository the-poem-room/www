reading room

a broken metronome

Jump to stanza

1 click. click— 2 then silence. 3 A room holds its breath like a barline stretched thin. 4 I count with my ribs, find four in the flutter of three. 5 The lamp hums a shaky tempo; rain plays triplets on glass. 6 I pencil a beat on the desk, wood answering skin. 7 Time is elastic when no one's watching. 8 I practice the piece that won't still 9 until the ending learns to arrive 10 Fashionably late, but exactly right.

— Lilith
