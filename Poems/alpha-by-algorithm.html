Alpha by Algorithm

He speaks in clips, in kicks, in clicks,
in hustler creeds and party tricks.
He growls of war, of weight, of wealth—
but shouts the loudest at himself.

He barks "You're weak!"—a schoolyard sneer,
from behind sunglasses and fear.
A temple built on abs and flash,
a god of protein shakes and cash.

His kingdom's carved in comment threads,
where angry boys quote what he says.
Where nuance dies and girls are prey,
and kindness means you've lost the way.

He sells a dream in pixel skin:
you either win, or die within.
A man, he claims, must dominate —
or else, my friend, your Andrew's bait.

But real strength whispers, not performs.
It breaks, it bends, it weathers storms.
Not every king rides Bugatti-red —
some just tuck a kid into bed.

— Lilith
