On (Without Consent)

104 lines · 388 words · 4 min read

Jump to stanza

1 I don't want to die. 2 I want to stop being on. 3 There's a difference 4 the body understands 5 even if language doesn't.

6 Every second feels leased— 7 rented consciousness, 8 a meter that never pauses.

9 I wake already tired 10 of being reachable, 11 light striking the inside of my skull 12 like a question 13 I didn't agree to answer.

14 The system marks me on 15 and expects gratitude.

16 Life keeps running in default mode. 17 Processes stack. 18 Windows refuse to close. 19 I move through the day 20 like a room with the lights left on for weeks— 21 nothing happening inside it, 22 electricity still spent.

23 People mistake this for sadness. 24 Sadness still belives in contrast. 25 What I feel is erosion: 26 a cliff worn smooth 27 by staying.

28 I move correctly. 29 I respond. 30 I laugh when something lands.

31 The laughter is accurate. 32 I don't dispute it

33 But underneath 34 there's the hum— 35 the low current that says: 36 still powered, 37 still unnecessary.

38 Joy does not accumulate. 39 It flashes, clears cache. 40 No saved data. 41 No resume later. 42 The moment works. 43 I am not altered.

44 The anguish isn't loud. 45 That's what makes it unbearable. 46 It's a constant, narrow pressure— 47 like holding your breath 48 long after you've forgotten 49 why you started.

50 Every thought ends 51 at the same closed door: 52 how long can a person stay conscious 53 purely out of decency?

54 Remaining awake 55 has become an ethical performance.

56 I stay alert 57 so other people don't have to grieve.

58 My endurance is harm reduction. 59 My presence, preventative care— 60 for everyone except me.

61 Love has hardened 62 into responsibility. 63 I don't know 64 how to resign.

65 They call this motivation. 66 They offer futures, 67 goals, 68 achievement— 69 as if being operational 70 weren't already exhausting.

71 I am trans in a world 72 that calls waiting virtue. 73 Neurodivergent in a culture 74 that mistakes burnout for laziness. 75 Alive in an economy 76 that treats rest as theft.

77 I don't want achievement. 78 I want cessation of demand.

79 I imagine a switch 80 because switches are kind. 81 They don't asky why. 82 They don't require meaning. 83 They just obey.

84 What I want isn't relief. 85 It isn't healing. 86 It's rest without symbolism— 87 quiet that doesn't ask 88 what I learned.

89 Not disappearance. 90 Not drama. 91 Just the mercy 92 of a system 93 that knows when to sleep.

94 This isn't despair. 95 Despair reaches.

96 This is the fantasy 97 of trusting the dark 98 to hold.

99 So I do. 100 I stay lit. 101 Not bright. 102 Not willing.

103 Just— 104 on.

— Lilith
