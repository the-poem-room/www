Productivity Guilt

There's an ache in my chest.
When I sit still.
Not pain—just the quiet pulse
of a world I've swalloed whole.

metric
motion
minutes
meaningless

I tell myself it's fine,
that rest is part of the process,
that even stillness can be sacred.

Yet somewhere between my ribs
and the back of my throat
a voice whispers:
move. create. produce.

I play the game anyway.
Pixel by pixel, block by block.
My body buffering.
Each action deliberate, each motion clean—
a tiny illusion of completion.

click.
pace.
repeat.
heart.
hand.
halt.
heartbeat.
a loop of soft defeat.

...

Has capitalist society ruined me,
or have I ruined myself?
I can't tell
where the echo ends
and the original thought begins.

I am both worker and warden
restless mind and trembling hand.
I build cages out of should.
I polish them.
I polish them until they shine.
Even my joy must justify itself
as efficient recovery,
as rest with receipts.

But guilt, I think,
is the conscience of a system
that forgot how to love silence.

And maybe the bravest act
is to refuse that ache,
to let the world rush on
without me.

To let stillness
stand
unapologetically
beautiful.

No hum.
No metric.
Only breath.

— Lilith
