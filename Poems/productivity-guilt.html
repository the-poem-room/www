Productivity Guilt

56 lines · 205 words · 3 min read

1 There's an ache in my chest. 2 When I sit still. 3 Not pain—just the quiet pulse 4 of a world I've swalloed whole.

5 metric 6 motion 7 minutes 8 meaningless

9 I tell myself it's fine, 10 that rest is part of the process, 11 that even stillness can be sacred.

12 Yet somewhere between my ribs 13 and the back of my throat 14 a voice whispers: 15 move. create. produce.

16 I play the game anyway. 17 Pixel by pixel, block by block. 18 My body buffering. 19 Each action deliberate, each motion clean— 20 a tiny illusion of completion.

21 click. 22 pace. 23 repeat. 24 heart. 25 hand. 26 halt. 27 heartbeat. 28 a loop of soft defeat.

29 ...

30 Has capitalist society ruined me, 31 or have I ruined myself? 32 I can't tell 33 where the echo ends 34 and the original thought begins.

35 I am both worker and warden 36 restless mind and trembling hand. 37 I build cages out of should. 38 I polish them. 39 I polish them until they shine. 40 Even my joy must justify itself 41 as efficient recovery, 42 as rest with receipts.

43 But guilt, I think, 44 is the conscience of a system 45 that forgot how to love silence.

46 And maybe the bravest act 47 is to refuse that ache, 48 to let the world rush on 49 without me.

50 To let stillness 51 stand 52 unapologetically 53 beautiful.

54 No hum. 55 No metric. 56 Only breath.

— Lilith
