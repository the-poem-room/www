Foil, Barcode, Tongue

Jump to stanza

1 Arthur twists the shaker. 2 Not king— 3         not man 4             just a barcode etched 5             into the foil of a snack pack 6         beside the word potassium.

7 It glints in static. 8 Clean hands. 9 Clean mouth. 10 The book opens on the wrong page 11 and says, 12         “This is the journey.” 13         (it lies in perfect grammar.)

14 The ape cradles a spoon like a relic. 15 He cannot read, 16 but draws constellations in the dirt 17 with stolen scissors 18 from a child who dreamed in cut-out stars.

19 PEGI warns: mild fear. suggestive confusion. 20 The tongue is unsaved— 21     a slug, 22     a map, 23     a weapon once named.

24 Arthur again— 25     this time: ape. 26     this time: error. 27     this time: 28         bookless, 29         tongueless, 30         blessedly 31             unclean.

32 Uncap me, says the shaker. 33 I am not your flavour. 34 I am not your cure. 35 I am not 36     what you hoped I’d taste like.

37 Arthur aga— 38 Arthur 39 (again)

40 PEGI 13: contains existential themes

41 I am already 42     spilled. 43 And still— 44     you wipe. 45 (You knew that when you picked up the shaker.)

— Lilith
