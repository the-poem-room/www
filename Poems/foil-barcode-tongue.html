Foil, Barcode, Tongue

Arthur twists the shaker.
Not king—
        not man
            just a barcode etched
            into the foil of a snack pack
        beside the word potassium.

It glints in static.
Clean hands.
Clean mouth.
The book opens on the wrong page
and says,
        “This is the journey.”
        (it lies in perfect grammar.)

The ape cradles a spoon like a relic.
He cannot read,
but draws constellations in the dirt
with stolen scissors
from a child who dreamed in cut-out stars.

PEGI warns: mild fear. suggestive confusion.
The tongue is unsaved—
    a slug,
    a map,
    a weapon once named.

Arthur again—
    this time: ape.
    this time: error.
    this time:
        bookless,
        tongueless,
        blessedly
            unclean.

Uncap me, says the shaker.
I am not your flavour.
I am not your cure.
I am not
    what you hoped I’d taste like.

Arthur aga—
Arthur
(again)

PEGI 13: contains existential themes

I am already
    spilled.
And still—
    you wipe.
(You knew that when you picked up the shaker.)

— Lilith
