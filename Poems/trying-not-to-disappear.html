Trying Not to Disappear

91 lines · 311 words · 4 min read

1 Quartz in the windowsill. 2 Lantern in the rain. 3 Velocity hidden inside 4 the murmur of trains.

5 Cinnamon drifting 6 through gravity-heavy rooms. 7 A loose thread 8 pulling an avalanche 9 from somebody's chest.

10 The harbour looked liminal 11 that evening — 12 cactus shadows 13 flickering across marble walls, 14 every detour glowing briefly 15 inside the spectrum of dusk.

16 I carried a hollow compass 17 wrapped in velvet, 18 its rust-coloured circuit 19 still humming softly 20 like meadow remembering 21 an echo.

22 Vinegar skies above the glacier. 23 a paradox held carefully 24 beside a chipped teacup. 25 Beyond the horizon 26 the smell of sandalwood 27 and the whistle of distant factories.

28 Every fragment 29 felt nebular. 30 Procelain hands 31 reading through static and wildfire, 32 through magnolia gardens 33 and pendulum clocks.

34 We wandered the labyrinth 35 collecting trinkets: 36 a daydream folded into a ripple, 37 a cobweb suspended 38 above an inkwell crescent, 39 thunder trapped in mosaic glass.

40 The jigsaw never finished. 41 An ember turning slowly 42 inside a carousel of origami birds. 43 Semaphore lights blinking 44 down abandoned laneways 45 during the tempest.

46 Somewhere, 47 a biscuit softening in tea. 48 A dandelion surviving 49 beside a satellite dish. 50 Crystal accordion music 51 floating through petrichor evenings 52 where fireflies gathered 53 around old chalkboards.

54 Your suitcase remained unopened 55 inside the cathedral hallway. 56 Raindrops trembling 57 against saffron windows. 58 A mirage inside a telescope. 59 Walnut tables 60 wet with drizzle.

61 The typewriter kept speaking 62 in blueprint syntax. 63 Candlesticks throwing echoes 64 onto the fountain walls 65 while the zeppelin drifted silently 66 through clockwork shadow.

67 Near the lighthouse 68 the riverbed glittered 69 with feather-light monsoon water. 70 Starlight caught 71 inside a paperclip 72 on the cobblestones.

73 A harpsichord somewhere upstairs. 74 Snowfall against the turntable hiss. 75 Corridors filling slowly 76 with waterfall sound. 77 An hourglass 78 tasting faintly of peppermint.

79 Then: 80 balloons against silhouettes, 81 copper vines around the vineyard gate, 82 children on trampolines 83 chasing moths 84 through parachute-coloured lanes.

85 And afterwards, only moonlight. 86 A hummingbird suspended 87 inside silk-dair air. 88 Smoke rising softly 89 from the chimney 90 like the word itself 91 trying not to disappear.

— Lilith
