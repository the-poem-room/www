When's the Last Time?

"When's the last time you sat and thought about your own mortality for a whole minute?"

56 lines · 189 words · 2 min read

Jump to stanza

1 You flipped the question like a coin 2 and watched me wait for it to land.

3 So I did.

4 One minute. 5 Long enough for tea to cool. 6 Short enough for a heart to stop.

7 At first— 8 a box. 9 Not wood. Not stone. 10 Just absence, 11 shaped by my mother's voice cracking.

12 Then the seconds began to drop. 13 Sixty. 14 Each a leaf spiralling down.

15 0:10 16 my dog, still warm in the earth

17 0:23 18 the bruise I swore was nothing.

19 0:37 20 the fast of fear— 21 metal and milk.

22 0:42 23 bargaining 24 into nothing.

25 0:49 26 clocks grow wings. 27 hover above me, 28 ticking like insects. 29 one lands. 30 counts down. 31 evaporates.

32 0:57 33 I remembered something 34 my grandmother once said— 35 that the soul lingers, 36 just for a moment, 37 to see if it mattered.

38 Maybe this was that moment. 39 Or its rehearsal.

40 1:00 41 I didn't cry. 42 Not until later, 43 when I looked up 44 and saw the world still scrolling, 45 laughing, 46 not counting at all.

47 I had just spent 48 a whole minute dying, 49 and no one noticed.

50 3:14 51 the timer glitched. 52 or I did. 53 I stood. 54 the chair 55 still held 56 my outline.

— Lilith
