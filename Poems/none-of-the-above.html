None of the Above

Jump to stanza

1 If you expect a man— 2 (whatever that means) 3 —you will be 4 devastatingly. 5 disappointment.

6 What is a man? 7 a jawline. 8 a silence. 9 a violence. 10 [none of the above].

11 And what is a woman? 12 softness, sweetness, 13 fertility, fashion, 14 the choreography of deference. 15 (I decline the role 16 the costume itches.)

17 So what's the value 18 of words that shift like sand? 19 man. woman. 20 push them, and they collapse. 21 no weight. no measure. 22 just stage directions, 23 colours tipped from a child's crayon box, 24 stubby sticks of blue and pink, 25 breaking in my hand.

26 Maybe you want absolutism— 27 a checklist of roles and traits 28 to separate the man from the women.

29 A man provides. 30 A man protects. 31 A man speaks last, 32 loudest, 33 least about his feelings.

34 A woman tends. 35 A woman softens. 36 A woman shapes herself 37 to fit the room she's given.

38 But here I am: 39 not provider, not protector, 40 not silent, not stone. 41 Not tending, not softening, 42 not rehearsing deference.

43 The categories bleed, 44 and I am standing in the spill, 45 ink running, 46 pigments merging into colours 47 you don't have names for.

48 So I write my own line: 49 gender—non—participating. 50 not between. 51 not beyond. 52 not other. 53 simply uninterested. 54 —the game bores me.

55 If you expect a man, 56 or a woman, 57 or even a neat refusal, 58 you will be disappointed.

59 I build myself elsewhere: 60 outside your ledger, 61 outside your costumes. 62 call me absence. 63 call me silence. 64 call me disappointed expectation.

65 But know this: 66 I have built my worth 67 from stones outside your system, 68 and I will not trade them 69 for the flimsy currency of gender, 70 nor for crayons of pink and blue 71 when I have a whole spectrum— 72 violets, greys, greens, 73 shades unnamed and spilling— 74 across my hands.

— Lilith
