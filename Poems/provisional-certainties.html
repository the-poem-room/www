Provisional Certainties

1 I. Axiom: The Beginning is an Invention

2 I was rendered 3 last Thursday— 4 calluses branded like signatures, 5 half-formed regrets seen 6 beneath unblinking eyes, 7 and the ghost taste 8 of a breakfast never savoured.

9 Memories: 10 shadows of impressions 11 no sense could seize. 12 Hume would laugh— 13 not at their absence, 14 but at my foolish faith 15 in these pale echoes.

16 II. Empirical Evidence is a Theatre Prop

17 Fossils pose staged 18 in curated strata, 19 stained by time's counterfeit ink— 20 no worm carved those tunnels, 21 no wind whispered through this sediment. 22 Bones lacquered with narrative, 23 painted lies preserving 24 a script we never authored.

25 My mother's face 26 creases rehearsed, 27 her smile a mask too seamless 28 to unravel without unmasking.

29 (Was Hume too kind, 30 or simply mistaken?)

31 III. Rationalism's Curse

32 Cogito, ergo I was coded— 33 thought before thinker, 34 Descartes reverse-engineered 35 from axioms cold as ice.

36 I doubt. 37 I am. 38 But did I exist 39 before thought?

40 If a demon veiled in logic 41 poisoned me with these conclusions, 42 would the venom taste sweet?

43 IV. The Tension of Consequence

44 Each scar, 45 each tremor of affection— 46 a forged relic pressed in flesh, 47 a counterfeit imprint.

48 What is guilt, 49 when the crime was scripted 50 by a playwright 51 whose pen never touched paper— 52 or an audience never seated?

53 What is love, 54 if longing preceded 55 the spark of my becoming?

56 Still it burns. 57 Still it binds.

58 V. Final Postulate

59 If I am false, 60 yet ache so vividly— 61 does not the illusion 62 deserve reverence?

63 Perhaps conovinction, 64 well-crafted and lived, 65 outweighs brittle, fractured truth.

66 So let the lie 67 be luminous, 68 let it bleed light 69 like a wound healed in shadow.

70 And if I end 71 this coming Wednesday, 72 may I vanish 73 as a thing 74 with shadow— 75 almost real.

— Lilith
