Taking Off the Label

60 lines · 384 words · 4 min read

1 She calls the year a bin of receipts— 2 small proofs of two people— 3 and tips it into the dark. 4 "Wasted," she says, and the word lands 5 like a lid.

6 I am still holding the label 7 that cinched my chest: boyfriend, 8 a collar that glittered then tightened 9 until breathing felt like a verdict. 10 Every time it buckled, I edited myself: 11 drafts on drafts, sharpened commas, 12 kindness proofread by a committe of friends. 13 My heart, meanwhile, ran laps.

14 Her anger is a locked door with music inside. 15 She says kindness can't be distance, 16 says a year can't vanish 17 and still take up this much space. 18 She will not accept love that survives 19 by changing its name.

20 We draw our maps in different ink. 21 For her, love is a promise with a roof— 22 rooms that stay when weather changes, 23 a word you don't move out of. 24 For me, love is a field of questions— 25 curiosity walking the fence line at dusk, 26 pockets full of found stones. 27 Two geographies, one argument about borders.

28 Friends gather like jurors. 29 Some will nod as she speaks, 30 some will hold me together with practical string. 31 Betwee us: the customs of touch— 32 who hugs whom, how often, 33 what a heart next to a name is worth 34 after the treaty breaks.

35 She lists what it cost: 36 begging, coming back, hell and its paperwork. 37 I list what remains: 38 the way laughter learned our corners, 39 how I still look for her in good news, 40 the fact that care doesn't switch off— 41 it just stops pretending to be a house key.

42 We negotiate the future in small print: 43 friend, maybe; close, probably not.

44 She will keep the world love behind glass; 45 I will stop saying it out loud 46 and let it be what it is— 47 quiet, stubborn, unmarketable.

48 Somewhere a calendar drops its pages like feathers. 49 Somewhere a boy takes off a label 50 and chooses to keep breathing, 51 to put his ear back to music and the page. 52 Somewhere a girl refuses erasure, 53 refuses to let effort be landfill.

54 We don't agree on the math. 55 But if a year can teach anything, 56 it's this: not all value is a ring, 57 not all mercy is staying, 58 not all endings are waste. 59 The door is still a door, even when it closes. 60 You can lean on it and rest.

— Lilith
