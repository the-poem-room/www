Cartography of Shadows

60 lines · 274 words · 3 min read

1 In Pyongyang, 2 avenues wide as silence, 3 monuments rehearsed into stone. 4 From above, the city glows with borrowed light, 5 but inside the dark, 6 families barter rice for whispers of truth. 7 Yet on festival days, 8 drums ripple through the streets, 9 neighbours share rice cakes, 10 laughter rising louder than fear. 11 And still— 12 everywhere, a heartbeat resists.

13 In Gaza, 14 walls rise higher than sky, 15 a child knots a kite from plastic bags, 16 launches it above the rubble, 17 a sky more forgiving than the earth. 18 Even here, weddings weave music through alleys, 19 children race between broken stones, 20 their joy as defiant as the kite. 21 And still— 22 everywhere, a heartbeat resists.

23 In Xinjiang, 24 prayers are confiscated, 25 languages folded shut, 26 identity stored in files 27 no one is meant to read. 28 Still, in secret, mothers hum old lullabies, 29 fathers pass down forbidden words, 30 families gather in hidden rooms 31 to mark the new year with flame and song. 32 And still— 33 everywhere, a heartbeat resists.

34 In Myanmar, 35 voices vanish into cells, 36 where time is rationed like stale bread 37 and hope is contraband. 38 Yet lanterns rise each season, 39 floating like prayers no soldier can catch, 40 carrying the wishes of those 41 who refuse to stop dreaming. 42 And still— 43 everywhere, a heartbeat resists.

44 Across oceans, 45 refugees cling to rafts, 46 saltwater carving psalms into skin. 47 In deserts, 48 bones whiten beneath an indifferent sun. 49 And when rafts reach shore, 50 strangers embrace as kin, 51 building homes from nothing 52 but shared survival and song. 53 And still— 54 everywhere, a heartbeat resists.

55 Not borders, not monuments, 56 not the silence of power— 57 only the pulse, 58 quiet as a drum beneath the floor of the world, 59 the same rhythm in every chest, 60 refusing to stop.

— Lilith
