Perception Completes the World

27 lines · 131 words · 2 min read

1 Magenta was never born of light, 2 yet it lives between red and blue. 3 It is the mind's invention of harmony, 4 a chord the world forgot to play.

5 The ear listens in colour, 6 the eyes hum in tone. 7 Like the note you hear— 8 a ghost born from the closeness of two others— 9 the ear dreaming 10 in mathematics and mercy.

11 We draw lions in stars, 12 hear words in the wind, 13 find stories in static, 14 and meaning in noise.

15 Everywhere, perception 16 reaches out like a hand, 17 touching the air before the wall, 18 completing the shape 19 before it's drawn.

20 Without our eyes, 21 magenta would fade back into logic. 22 Without our ears, 23 the phantom tones would fall apart.

24 Maybe that's the secret: 25 reality hums in duet, 26 and we are the missing voice 27 that lets it sing.

— Lilith
