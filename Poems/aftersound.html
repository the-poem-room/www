reading room

aftersound

sometimes i forget
the shape of your voice
how silence
learned to sound like you

the air between us
has stretched so thin
it hums

sometimes the distance
outgrows the body
and what was once
a voice
becomes aftersound

i try to remember
how the air felt
when you were near
the texture of your eyes
how light used to rest there
like a hand on water

but months pile quietly
like dust on a table
and the room kept breathing
as time does its slow work
softening the edges

you become less person
more echo
until touch is a rumour
and names are only weather

i think of you
and it feels like thinking
of a place that still stands
but only in memory's map

the walls are still there
in my mind
but the paint flakes
the windows blur

and still
on certain nights
you flicker
like breath against glass
and for a moment

i almost believe
in form again

— Lilith
