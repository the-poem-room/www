The Third Room

Jump to stanza

1 I ate a banana and some raisins 2 and the world blinked.

3 Not loudly, 4 not with thunder or colour, 5 but with that quiet click 6 of something new arriving, 7 like stepping into 8 the third room.

9 I know banana. 10 I know raisin. 11 Soft curve of yellow, 12 wrinkled sweetness of dried sun. 13 Two rooms in my mind 14 I've visited before.

15 But together, 16 they made a third room 17 with no door I could have pictured.

18 My tongue learned something 19 my brain could not rehearse: 20 a small, uncanny future 21 inside my own mouth.

22 I thought imagination 23 was a wide enough field 24 to hold every outcome.

25 But the flavour said — 26 no.

27 There are things you only meet 28 when they happen.

29 And for a moment, 30 I wasn't just tasting fruit, 31 I was tasting the limit 32 of being human.

— Lilith
