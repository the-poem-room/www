The Butterfly's Lover

after Lorenz, after you

Jump to stanza

1 It didn't begin with love. 2 Not fate. Not fate. 3 Just a staggered queue. 4 Just air being air. 5 A door left ajar. 6 A half-stirred coffee. 7 A cloud dragging too slowly over a supermarket car park. 8 The decision to wear blue instead of green. 9 A gust of wind rerouted by scaffolding. 10 A mother forgetting her keys. 11 A boy choosing to walk.

12 It began with evrything 13 that didn't know 14 it was beginning us.

15 You, 16 three streets away, 17 looked up at a sound you'll never remember. 18 I knelt to tie my shoe; 19 someone crossed instead of me. 20 The weather recalculated. 21 The system found a new attractor 22 in the sliver of space between 23 your path and mine.

24 We like to think 25 love is a clean story— 26 two dots, 27 connected by desire and timing. 28 But desire is just data. 29 Timing is turbulence. 30 And the dots 31 have always been infinite— 32 looping in fractals, 33 never settling, 34 patterns nearly resolving 35 then splitting again.

36 I have loved you 37 through weather reports 38 and missed buses, 39 through forgotten umbrellas 40 and delays at junction four, 41 through the soft migration 42 of monarchs 43 and the tremble of a coin 44 landing on its edge.

45 Chaos means the world cannot be rewound. 46 It means a shift in the sixth decimal 47 can tilt a system 48 toward drought, 49 toward music, 50 toward you. 51 It means feedback loops 52 where a sigh 53 in an elevator 54 becomes a revolution. 55 A glance 56 becomes a war. 57 A breath 58 becomes a sonata. 59 A blush 60 becomes a decade.

61 You sat beside me 62 on a bench 63 that didn't know it would be holy. 64 You laughed— 65 like a wind chime 66 finally catching the note 67 it had waited for all winter.

68 When your fingers brushed mine, 69 the cuture collapsed inward. 70 Somewhere, 71 a glacier moved a fraction slower. 72 A fire delayed its ignition. 73 A stranger picked up the phone. 74 A child was spared.

75 I only know 76 how your palm 77 made a cathedral of my hand. 78 How your voice 79 rewrote my breath 80 without asking. 81 You are 82 the strange attractor 83 of my every future. 84 I orbit you 85 the way tide obeys moon— 86 not from love, 87 but pull.

88 There are versions of this life 89 where you leave the café 90 five seconds earlier— 91 and I pass the bench alone. 92 Versions 93 where I never speak, 94 where I only dream 95 of the echo 96 of a laugh 97 I never heard.

98 And even in the one 99 where the butterfly 100 chooses the tulip, 101 where the scaffolding holds the wind 102 a little longer than it should— 103 I like to think 104 some part of me 105 still turns, 106 still waits, 107 still knows 108 that something, 109 somewhere, 110 almost began.

111 So I will spend 112 every heartbeat I am given 113 leaving offerings 114 at the altar of every almost— 115 for the door left ajar, 116 for the coin that wobbled, 117 for the storm that never came, 118 for the butterfly 119 that never knew my name 120 and saved me anyway.

— Lilith
