Hedonic Calculus

Jump to stanza

1 This morning, 2 I wanted toast. 3 Bentham whispers: weigh the pleasures, weigh the pains.

4 Pleasure: warmth, crunch, 5 jam dripping off the edge like a sunrise. 6 Pain: sticky fingers, 7 crumbs in my bed for the next three weeks.

8 I assign numbers, 9 pretend to be objective. 10 Who knew breakfast came out at +4.9 units of happiness? 11 (The decimals make it philosophy.)

12 By lunch, 13 chips or fruit. 14 Chips: salt, joy, +7 15 but greasy fingers (-2) 16 and vinegar stinging a paper cut (-3) 17 Fruit: refreshing, healthy, +5, 18 but sometimes the banana betrays me with brown spots (-4).

19 I calculate so long 20 the chips go cold. 21 Net pleasure: –∞.

22 Soon I'm rating everything. 23 Brushing my teeth? +3, unless the tap's icy, then -1. 24 Crossing the road? +1 if safe, -70 if flattened. 25 Scrolling my phone? +2 for content, -5 for reading the comments.

26 By evening, 27 my brain is a spreadsheet— 28 with trust issues.

29 And then. 30 The thought. 31 The unspeakable thought:

32 Should I do 33 a hedonic calculus 34 on whether doing hedonic calculuses 35 is even worth it?

36 Intesity: headache. 37 Duration: eternal. 38 Certainty: 100%. 39 Fecundity: terrifying— 40 calculating the pleasure 41 of calculating the pleasure 42 of calculating the pleasure...

43 Somewhere in the loop, 44 I picture Jeremy Bentham, 45 propped in his glass box at UCL, 46 his real body stuffed in a chair, 47 his wax head staring like a bad museum exhibit. 48 He nods politely— 49 then sighs, 50 because he never meant this. 51 It was just a neat framework, 52 a scaffold for ethics, 53 not a way to rate toast like it's Eurovision.

54 So I give up, 55 rate the whole day as "medium, could be better," 56 and wonder what rating 57 Bentham would give 58 his glass-box life.

— Lilith
