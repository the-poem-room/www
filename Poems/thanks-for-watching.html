Thanks for Watching

for October 21st, 2023

28 lines · 164 words · 2 min read

Jump to stanza

1 I didn't visit much anymore, 2 not like those days when pixel suns 3 rose with his voice — warm and sure — 4 and the world has built for fun.

5 I'd long since left the treehouses, 6 the cake trails and dogs he named, 7 grown through those stories, 8 with real-world things to claim.

9 But still, the morning lingered 10 in some soft-lit part of me, 11 wooden planks, a gentle laugh, 12 the place I first felt free.

13 And then — the end. 14 Not sudden, not unkind. 15 Just a bow, a breath, a final sign-off 16 from that world I'd left behind.

17 He: "Thanks for watching." 18 and the years came rushing through — 19 how endings slip in quietly, 20 with a whispered, "meant for you."

21 He closed a door I thought had vanished, 22 long boarded up with age, 23 but it opened, just to wave goodbye, 24 from that distant, golden stage.

25 And I? I wept for something soft, 26 for something small yet bright — 27 a world that once had held me close, 28 then let me go, just right.

— Lilith
