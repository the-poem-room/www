reading room

tolerate

61 lines · 272 words · 3 min read

1 tolerate is an ugly word. 2 it chews on itself. 3 it stares through glass and calls it empathy.

4 accept, though— 5 that one breathes. 6 that one smells of rain and static electricity. 7 it doesn't ask the air for permission.

8 but— 9 what if the air burns?

10 I tell myself: be open 11 listen. 12 no, not like that— 13 listen like a scientist dissecting kindness.

14 in theory, 15 I could discuss the ethics of pain 16 while someone screams. 17 in theory, 18 I could find beauty 19 in the architecture of cruelty.

20 but theory drips when held too tightly. 21 and the scream becomes a language 22 I cannot mistranslate.

23 tolerate. 24 the world keeps crawling back. 25 it smells like disinfectant, 26 like a peace treaty written in smog.

27 I don't want to tolerate. 28 I want to accept the chaos of existing— 29 to bow to difference 30 without bowing to harm.

31 (you can't, says a voice. 32 you must draw lines.)

33 so I do. 34 I say: murder is wrong. 35 freedom is sacred. 36 autonomy, non-negotiable. 37 I mean it. 38 and I hate that I mean it 39 with the same fervour 40 I hate the word tolerate.

41 ethical emotivism— 42 a nice label 43 for a heart that argues with itself.

44 sometimes I imagine 45 morality as an organ humming in my chest, 46 detuned, self-aware. 47 it keeps playing the wrong note 48 and calling it compassion.

49 I want to be kind. 50 I want to be right. 51 I want to stop wanting both.

52 but the truth is— 53 different doesn't need my consent. 54 the world will go on fracturing 55 whether I forgive it or not.

56 so I practice acceptance, 57 not of all things, 58 but of this: 59 that I am not the centre 60 and still, 61 I burn like one.

— Lilith
