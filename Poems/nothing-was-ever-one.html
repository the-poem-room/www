Nothing Was Ever One

(a Meditation on Mereological Nihilism)

Jump to stanza

1 The table is not.

2 It is wood-bits arranged table-wise, 3 each grain a whisper from what once grew, 4 each fibre aligned, 5 not belonging—only nearby.

6 The chair beneath me 7 does not hold— 8 not truly. 9 It is parts pretending posture, 10 matter shaped comfort-wise, 11 but never one.

12 We declare:

13 This is a cup.

14 We lift it. 15 Sip. 16 Trust its shape to hold warmth. 17 But the whole disagrees. 18 It is ceramics arranged cup-wise.

19 The cloud? 20 A mist of molecules. 21 No thing. 22 Only movement.

23 We call it a wave, 24 but no ocean holds it. 25 We name it a kiss, 26 but no atom yields. 27 Even pressed close, 28 they remain alone.

29 They do not touch. 30 Not truly.

31 Each simple— 32 not part, 33 not piece, 34 just itself.

35 No merging 36 no mingling— 37 only nearness mistaken 38 for embrace.

39 Your hand— 40 so sure, so warm— 41 is a fiction of form. 42 SImples clustered hand-like, 43 never touching, 44 never held.

45 A stone in your hand. 46 The hand itself. 47 Neither whole, 48 neither one. 49 Only nearness. 50 Only names.

51 And the apple— 52 red, round, real?

53 No.

54 Taste is chemistry. 55 Crunch is structure. 56 It is not apple— 57 only atoms, 58 arranged convincingly.

59 We see a forest— 60 but ony trees. 61 We see a body— 62 but only blood, bones, breath, 63 never bound.

64 Even breath, 65 that rhythm we dare call ours, 66 is air in motion— 67 briefly arranged.

68 If you could fall 69 deep into any thing— 70 a tree, a coin, a breath— 71 you'd find no hidden centre. 72 No core waiting underneath.

73 Each simple, 74 without union. 75 Notes in a chord 76 never struck.

77 Still, 78 we hunger for the whole. 79 We draw outlines around the world 80 and pretend they stay still.

81 The mind rebels against 82 what it cannot hold. 83 It wraps the fog 84 and calls it form.

85 And I— 86 I am not. 87 Not a self, 88 not a soul. 89 Only flesh-patterned momentarily. 90 Simples behaving person-wise.

91 Yet sometimes, 92 in the quiet, 93 the illusion falters— 94 a name slips, 95 a shape stutters, 96 and the thing we grasp 97 is gone.

98 Nothing was ever truly one.

99 Not the table. 100 Not the wave. 101 Not the body. 102 Not the grief. 103 Not the self.

104 The world does not fall apart.

105 It never was together.

106 A house of cards 107 is still a pile of cards 108 before the wind.

109 Let it fall.

110 Let the cup un-cup, 111 the self un-self, 112 the shape dissolve 113 from the thing it tried to hold.

114 There is no loss. 115 Only release.

116 You were never whole. 117 And that is no tragedy. 118 What is not one 119 cannot break.

120 Even grief changes— 121 when the thing it mourns 122 was never one.

123 Feel the air of it— 124 the lightness 125 of not being bound. 126 Not to name. 127 Not to form. 128 Not to permanence.

129 You are not a body. 130 You are not a thought. 131 You are a moment of arrangement, 132 passing through possibility.

133 And in that, 134 a quieter kind of peace.

135 No gods above. 136 No essence within. 137 No edge where the self ends.

138 Only the soft drift 139 of what never needed holding.

— Lilith
