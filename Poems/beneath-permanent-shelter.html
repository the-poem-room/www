Beneath Permanent Shelter

166 lines · 642 words · 7 min read

Jump to stanza

1 A year is a strangely human way 2 to measure absence.

3 The trees outside my window 4 have already forgotten 5 which leaves belonged 6 to last spring.

7 But today marks exactly 8 three hundred and sixty-five days 9 since I last beside you 10 without needing to explain myself.

11 And I still wake some mornings 12 with the shape of you 13 like an indentation 14 in fabric.

15 Not love — 16 at least not the kind 17 the world keeps trying to sell us.

18 Something quieter. 19 Something harder to name 20 without sounding naïve.

21 Now, my days are cleaner.

22 That is the first truth.

23 I eat better. 24 Sleep earlier. 25 Write more. 26 Thinkn further.

27 My room no longer smells 28 of stale afternoons 29 left to rot into midnight.

30 I have learned how to sit alone 31 without immediately reaching 32 for another voice 33 to fill the weather inside me.

34 Sometimes I walk for hours 35 through cold streets 36 with music in my headphones 37 and feel almost unbearable gratitude 38 for my own mind.

39 A year ago 40 I could not do that.

41 A year ago 42 I was living almost entirely 43 beneath shelter.

44 You were a harbour 45 I mistook for the sea.

46 And I stayed anchored there 47 so long 48 I forgot currents existed.

49 We built our friendship 50 like children building dens 51 inside the roots of enormous trees — 52 safe beneath the dark, 53 warm with breath and laughter, 54 certain the storm could never touch us.

55 And maybe it couldn't.

56 But nothing grows 57 beneath permanent shelter.

58 There are whole forests 59 that only open 60 after fire.

61 I think of us then —

62 the shared mattress on my floor, 63 the strange ease of our bodies, 64 how neither of us flinched 65 from skin or softness.

66 The way you hugged me 67 without calculation.

68 How I could exist beside you 69 without performing personhood 70 quite so aggressively.

71 Because that is what most days are, 72 still.

73 Performance.

74 Voice measured. 75 Hands measured. 76 Every gesture edited 77 through the static ache 78 of living inside a body 79 that feels partly 80 mistranslated.

81 People exhaust me 82 the way bright supermarkets do — 83 too much light, 84 too much awareness 85 of being perceived.

86 But with you 87 I could forget my outline.

88 I could be unfinished aloud.

89 And maybe that was part 90 of the danger.

91 Not you.

92 Not even us.

93 Just the temptation 94 to remain hidden forever 95 inside something warm.

96 I miss that warmth still, 97 with a grief 98 that arrives softly, 99 like fog crossing fields 100 rather than storms.

101 However —

102 I cannot pretend 103 we were saving each other.

104 You were drifting outward already.

105 Toward girlfriends, 106 toward future 107 shaped like escape velocity.

108 And I — 109 terrified of becoming real — 110 held tighter 111 to what was easy.

112 You became a room 113 where I never had to ask 114 who I might become 115 if left alone long enough.

116 Ivy climbed quietly there.

117 By the time I noticed, 118 it knew the walls 119 better than I did.

120 I do not blame you 121 for wanting more life 122 than our little orbit allowed.

123 Sometimes I wonder 124 if I made you smaller too.

125 If we both mistook closeness 126 for completion.

127 The world teaches boys 128 to abandon tenderness 129 the moment it risks being mistaken 130 for need.

131 It teaches all of us 132 that intimacy only matters 133 when romance sanctifies it.

134 As though two people 135 cannot hold each other together 136 briefly and beautifully 137 without eventually being asked 138 what they are becoming.

139 Maybe we broke partly beneath 140 that pressure.

141 Or maybe we simply reached 142 the natural edge 143 of who we were to each other.

144 Some rivers run beside on another 145 for miles through valleys 146 before quietly dividing 147 around separate hills.

148 Neither river is wrong 149 for continuing.

150 Still, today, 151 one year later, 152 I stood in the kitchen 153 unable to decide 154 what to do 155 with my hands.

156 And for a moment 157 I missed the simplicity 158 of existing beside you 159 more than anything else 160 I have ever known.

161 But I also opened the window.

162 And outside, 163 the wind moved freely 164 through branches no longer tied together.

165 It sounded lonely.

166 It sounded alive.

— Lilith
