The Angle of Light

23 lines · 102 words · 2 min read

1 I no longer call it sacred, 2 yet I still pause 3 when light meets glass 4 at the exact angle 5 that turns precision into silence.

6 They say mystery sustains meaning, 7 but I have learned 8 that exposure is its own devotion— 9 the more I understand, 10 the more atonished I become 11 by what understanding cannot hold.

12 To name the atom 13 does not still its shimmer. 14 To measure the sky 15 does not steal its blue. 16 Awe endures dissection; 17 it only learns 18 to breathe without illusion.

19 There is holiness, still, 20 in the honest perimeter of knowing, 21 in the mind that burns 22 to see clearly, 23 and does not kneel.

— Lilith
