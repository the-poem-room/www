We Already Know the Song

Jump to stanza

1 Each day I sit at the piano, 2 not to practise, 3 not to perform, 4 but to listen 5 to what the air remembers.

6 A note, 7 then another. 8 Between them: a question. 9 Between them: a pulse.

10 The fingers know before I do. 11 They wander, curious, 12 through sound that feels 13 as if it's been waiting 14 to be found again.

15 It feels formless, 16 but maybe form is only what's left 17 after freedom forgets itself.

18 Sometimes the shapes repeat, 19 slowly, deliberately, 20 patterns breathing until they disappear. 21 Sometimes they reach too far, 22 colliding colours in eleven, 23 and smile at their own surprise. 24 Sometimes they open their arms, 25 and everyone fits inside.

26 Bb7#11 blooms under my hand, 27 a chord like sunlight through broken glass. 28 I could name it — 29 but that's only the shadow 30 of what it means to be inside a sound 31 before it names itself.

32 The wrist, the breath, the spine — 33 they remember more than thought. 34 A body listens faster than the mind. 35 A pattern hums behind the pattern, 36 a logic that never needed language.

37 Maybe freedom is just the feeling 38 of inevitability, moving gracefully. 39 Maybe wonder is how knowledge 40 learns to breathe.

41 I play, and think how 42 every note I touch 43 is also someone else's. 44 How my quiet room 45 belongs to a wider choir 46 I can't quite hear.

47 A mother's hum, 48 a child's drum, 49 rain rhythm on a roof — 50 all of it inside this chord, 51 and inside me. 52 For the chord is not a thing I build 53 but a place I become. 54 Each echo I've ever gathered 55 waits beneath my hands: 56 every voice I've loved, 57 every silence I've learned, 58 every ordinary sound 59 that taught me how to listen. 60 I am the instrument remembering itself, 61 and this note, 62 this moment, 63 this trembling yes, 64 is everything I am.

65 No one teaches this. 66 We only remember. 67 It rises in us 68 like the tide remembers the moon.

69 Listen: 70 a thousand strangers find the same note 71 by accident and by love. 72 They never call it art. 73 they just sing.

74 Even when the harmony bends, 75 when tension grits the air, 76 we stay. 77 Because dissonance, too, carries kindness: 78 it leans toward resolution 79 like thought toward understanding, 80 like strangers learning 81 to listen without needing to agree. 82 Beauty lives there, 83 in the ache that hums beneath resolve, 84 in the shimmer before it settles.

85 Because art was never the thing itself, 86 only the noticing, 87 the shimmer between perception and breath, 88 the world meeting the skin of attention.

89 Harmony is what logic sounds like 90 when it learns to feel. 91 Dissonance, what feeling sounds like 92 when it dares to think. 93 Beauty is the body's way 94 of saying yes to being alive.

95 Music begins the moment permission is given: 96 permission to listen, 97 to wonder, 98 to belong, 99 to lean into what jars 100 until it becomes part of the song.

101 A circle, 102 a breath, 103 a home that sounds like everyone.

104 And tomorrow I'll sit again, 105 not to practise, 106 not to perform, 107 but to listen, 108 for the silence between the notes, 109 and for the tension 110 that teaches them to sing.

— Lilith
