reading room

Fuck ICE

1 Let's not do the cable-news dance. 2 Let's not stand at the podium, 3 beneath the flags and the blue-lit gloss, 4 and pretend this is difficult.

5 It isn't.

6 You took a country already swollen 7 with grievance, spectacle, surveillance; 8 fet it donor money, 9 powdered its face for television, 10 then taught it to scroll.

11 Now every soft-handed strongman 12 with a flag pin, a ring light, and a media coach 13 can go online and speak of 14 the border crises, 15 law and order, 16 illegals, 17 as though a family could be crushed to a caption, 18 as though fear becomes fact 19 if it travels fast.

20 Here is the scam:

21 a billionaire writes the cheque. 22 a think tank writes the script. 23 a senator reads it slowly into a microphone 24 like he thought of it himself. 25 some stream-fed pundit trims it to a clip. 26 the algorithm smells blood 27 and calls it content.

28 Then, somewhere beneath all that polish, 29 a front door opens 30 into hell.

31 Because the people who build this machine 32 never stand in the doorway. 33 They live too high up.

34 They live among cufflinks, cocktails, studio powder, 35 green rooms, golf grass, private flights. 36 Their shoes do not stick to stairwells. 37 Their hands do not shake at knocks. 38 Words like deterrence, 39 enforcement, 40 removal 41 float cleanly from one soft mouth to another, 42 never once passing through the throat 43 of the person they mean to erase.

44 But down here, 45 those words arrive with boots in them.

46 Down here, they mean 47 rent still due on Friday; 48 a half-zipped school bag by the sofa; 49 lunch still packed in the fridge; 50 steam lifting from rice; 51 a mother reaching, still reaching, 52 for her child, 53 while some armed bureaucract, 54 funded by men who hoard tax cuts like trophies, 55 turns her life into a case number—

56 and someone else 57 turns it into reach.

58 That may be the filthiest part of it: 59 not just the raid, 60 but the feed.

61 The stitched reaction. 62 The smirk. 63 The flag in the bio. 64 The patriot in the comment section, 65 typing send them back, 66 with one hand 67 while the other hovers at the screen 68 as if it might finally hand him 69 the jawline, the woman, the rank 70 he thinks the world forgot to give him.

71 All this chest-thumping content. 72 All this borrowed "masculinity." 73 All this synthetic manhood, 74 bottled, branded, backlit— 75 men taught to confuse cruelty with discipline, 76 contempt with strength, 77 humiliation with truth.

78 Not grief, but grift.

79 And spare me the fake seriousness. 80 The debate-bro cadence. 81 The "just asking questions." 82 The smarmy little pause 83 before the next rehearsed evasion. 84 All that confidence, 85 all that posturing, 86 all that edge lord ironed flat for advertisers.

87 Because oligarchy does not only speak 88 from podiums now.

89 Now it livestreams. 90 Now it quote-tweets. 91 Now it buries xenophobia in memes, 92 wraps it in irony, 93 slips it between ads for razors and supplements, 94 and lets the algorithm do the marching.

95 ICE is no longer just an agency. 96 It is a format:

97 Cuffs and chyrons, 98 dashboard footage and atriotic music, 99 a press release shaved down to a clip, 100 a bureaucracy of terror 101 made slick enough to scroll past.

102 And the same old men still profit.

103 The Super PAC banquet. 104 The DHS memo. 105 The panel segment. 106 The executive order. 107 The detention contract. 108 The van. 109 The viral clip. 110 The ad revenue.

111 All of it connected. 112 Spotless on paper. 113 Filthy where it lands.

114 They always do this— 115 the men in tailored fascism.

116 They bury the wound 117 under acronyms, 118 under hashtags, 119 under bullet points, 120 under thumbnails, 121 until the blood dries into discourse;

122 then they call it debate.

123 As if a nation were a comment section. 124 As if cruelty were just another take. 125 As if the poor were tinder, 126 and brown skin, tired eyes, an accent, a bus ticket, 127 could be arranged into a spectacle 128 bright enough, loud enough, 129 to distract from the men 130 selling off the republic, room by room.

131 Fuck ICE.

132 Fuck every donor-fat patriot, 133 every smooth-faced apparatchik, 134 every hedge-fund nationalist, 135 every blue-check coward, 136 every studio-bred prophet of "strength" 137 who says secure the border 138 from perfect lighting 139 and has never once had to wonder 140 whether the knock at the door 141 was meant for them.

142 One day the branding will fail. 143 The posts will sink. 144 The chyrons will fade. 145 The talking points will curdle in the mouth.

146 And those grave little phrases— 147 national emergency, 148 strong borders, 149 rule of law, 150 America First— 151 will collapse back into what they always were:

152 money deciding 153 who gets hunted.

154 History will not remember 155 the segment titles 156 or the poll numbers.

157 It will remember the splintered door. 158 The phone lit up on the counter. 159 The rice gone cold. 160 The child gone quiet.

161 And somewhere far away, 162 a man adjusting his tie 163 before going on air 164 to call it order.

— Lilith
