How Do You Want Me?

Jump to stanza

1 I ask, and you say: 2 "Just be yourself." 3 As if I haven't spent years 4 twisting into versions 5 of me you might want.

6 I hold your hand.

7 And wonder 8 if I'm too much— 9 or not enough.

10 You say, "continue as normal," 11 but normal, for me, 12 is overthinking 13 until my chest hurts.

14 I want to know 15 if you want me 16 to speak softer, 17 or lead louder; 18 to chase you in silence 19 or wait in the open.

20 Do you want me 21 unfolded and tender? 22 Or wrapped in riddle and edge? 23 Do you want 24 a fire to warm you 25 or a forest to lose you?

26 Do you want me 27 to tease and surprise you, 28 or be calm, reliable, 29 the one who always texts back?

30 You say, "nothing's wrong." 31 But if nothing's wrong, 32 why do I feel like 33 a lock with no key?

34 I love you. 35 That's the part I knw. 36 But loving 37 without instructions 38 feels like trying to dance 39 to music I can't hear— 40 scouring your texts 41 for clues 42 I can't decode.

43 Tell me, 44 how do you want me?

45 Because I'm trying 46 to be myself— 47 but I don't know 48 which self is still mine 49 after so many years of 50 trying to be 51 what you need.

— Lilith
