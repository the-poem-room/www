A Note Outside the Scale

61 lines · 269 words · 3 min read

1 I am not a man, 2 nor a woman— 3 nor half of one pretending to be whole. 4 I am GNP: 5 a balance, an absence, 6 a body that speaks in shifting pitches, 7 sometimes velvet-deep, 8 sometimes bright as flutes.

9 My hair is clean, tied, blonde— 10 a kind of rebellion against the buzz-cut of expectation. 11 My voice is a compass, 12 shifting with the room, 13 with the ears 14 that would try to name me.

15 The world of hetero eyes 16 is a hall of mirrors. 17 Every glance a ranking, 18 every conversation a ledger 19 ticking "hot or not," 20 measured by rules 21 that collapse when touched. 22 Boys: praised for conquest. 23 Girls: chastised unless 24 they are both unbroken porcelain 25 and marked by use. 26 A cruel arithmetic 27 I refuse to play.

28 But with lesbians, 29 I breathe. 30 They do not script me 31 into a part I never auditioned for. 32 They hear my laughter, 33 not its register. 34 They see my body 35 without turning it into battlefield: 36 what I should be, 37 who should I want, 38 how many I should have won.

39 Perhaps they know— 40 from living outside the story 41 that was written without them— 42 that freedom feels like 43 an unscored passage. 44 That kindness can grow 45 where expectation does not root.

46 With lesbians, I am treated better, 47 because they do not mistake me 48 for a symbol of their desire 49 or a soldier of tradition. 50 They meet me where I am: 51 a voice that wavers, 52 a body I keep clean, 53 a mind hungry for ideas 54 a soul refusing the ledger.

55 And in their presence, 56 I am not unfinished, 57 not miscast, 58 not half. 59 I am exactly enough— 60 a note outside the scale 61 that makes music.

— Lilith
