NEUROSPAGHETTI

Jump to stanza

1 I. BOOT SEQUENCE 2     I spoke in metaphor 3     before I could tie my laces. 4     Other kids played. 5     I did 100-piece jigsaws, 6     upside down, 7     for fun. 8     I was three, 9     asking how 10     the universe began 11     and why grown-ups lied 12     with their eyes. 13     I knew. 14     I knew. 15     My brain was not built 16     to follow instructions.

17 II. TABS OPEN 18     [] email miss re: ensemble clash 19     [] that bird in Year 4 that nodded at me 20     [] 17-minute bass solo (notated in back of maths book?) 21     [] how to sound curious without sounding condescending 22     [] what even is a normal sleep schedule 23     [] define “pedantic” + synonyms that don’t sting 24     [] Bedwars: low-ground clutch strat (test at 3am) 25     [] tone of your voice when you said “I miss you” 26     [] oven status: Schrödinger’s preheat 27     [] mental inbox: 374 unread 28     popup: your brain has encountered an error. 29     click [continue anyway]

30 III. HYPERFOCUS 31     “Just five minutes.” 32     That’s what I told myself. 33     And then: 34     a chord, 35     a line, 36     a thirty-seven-layer polyphonic cathedral 37     rising out of muscle memory and hunger. 38     I forgot to eat. 39     I forgot to move. 40     But the counterpoint 41     was divine.

42 IV. STATIC SPIRAL 43     The light too 44     bright 45     the room too full 46     shirt like needles 47     air like shouting 48     stillness like punishment 49     The ceiling hums a C# — 50     and I’m the only one who hears it. 51     I forget my body. 52     I remember everything.

53 V. TRANSLATION TABLE 54     when I say: 55     “That’s not quite right.” 56     I mean: 57     “Let’s wonder deeper.” 58     when I say: 59     “Technically…” 60     I mean: 61     “Isn’t truth complicated and beautiful?” 62     But they hear: 63     “You’re wrong.” 64     “You’re difficult.” 65     “You’re trying to win.” 66     When I say: 67     “Actually—“ 68     they flinch. 69     I wanted to say: 70     “Please trust that I care.”

71 VI. ECHO CHAMBER 72     I remember: 73     the exact angle of sun on my Year 2 workbook, 74     the pen-click rhythm from the boy behind me in Year 7, 75     the scientific name of the laughing owl (now extinct). 76     But I forget: 77     birthdays. 78     lessons. 79     messages. 80     to eat. 81     what I meant to say 82     mid-sentence.

83 VII. TIME SIGNATURES 84     on meds: 85     quiet 86     order 87     a room with all the doors shut 88     and no music inside. 89     no wrong notes — 90     but no melody, either. 91     off meds: 92     cacophony, sure 93     but full of colour 94     and risk 95     and me. 96     some weeks: 97     5am jogs 98     and timetables drawn in pen. 99     others: 100     3am arranging binges 101     and a sunrise lullaby 102     for no one.

103 VIII. PSALM FOR THE STORM 104     I am not a glitch. 105     Not a system to patch. 106     I’m a symphony of misfired sparks 107     and exact metaphors, 108     of overwhelming floods 109     and holy obsessions. 110     I know I crash. 111     I know I drop things. 112     I know I burn 113     brighter 114     than I meant to. 115     But I would rather burn 116     than dim.

117 IX. LOWERCASE PRAYER 118     i love my brain. 119     even when it betrays me. 120     even when it drops every plate 121     just to show me how they shatter. 122     i love it 123     for the music 124     the logic 125     the laughter 126     the way it builds galaxies 127     from conversation scraps. 128     i love it 129     even when it does not 130     love me back. 131     and still, i begin again. 132     and still, i begin again. 133     i spoke in metaphor…

— Lilith
