The Wheel Doesn't Know Why You're Here

Jump to stanza

1 It spins 2 without memory.

3 Plastic light 4 clicks past corridors 5 painted by dying children, 6 past ice-cream vans 7 parked outside grief.

8 A man with soft-serve hands 9 stands very still, 10 holding a hospice 11 between his ribs.

12 Derian House 13 waits quietly 14 off-camera.

15 He asks a question 16 about films.

17 Wrong.

18 The wheel does not flinch. 19 It has never heard of hospice beds, 20 or morphine, 21 or children learning 22 they will not grow up.

23 It only knows 24 answers.

25 Another chair turns.

26 A woman who has learned 27 how to survive her own cells 28 steps forward 29 carrying Maggie's 30 like a small candle 31 that could change 32 someone else's dark.

33 Lucy speaks. 34 The answer is wrong. 35 Seventy-nine thousand pounds 36 evaporates 37 without ceremony.

38 Trending headline: 39 "Comedian costs cancer charity £79,000."

40 The machine 41 does not apologise.

42 Instead, a human hand 43 reaches into a real pocket — 44 an appearance fee, 45 a flawed, beautiful interruption.

46 Money given 47 not by the system 48 but in spite of it.

49 Proof that the wheel 50 was never the only way.

51 They call this fairness 52 because everyone was treated equally.

53 Equally unseen. 54 Equally unheard.

55 This is what fairness looks like 56 when you remove 57 context.

58 And when the wheel does stop 59 on something bright, 60 something generous, 61 something big —

62 the body learns 63 its ritualised gesture.

64 Hands shake. 65 Arms open. 66 Performed reverence.

67 The host is embraced 68 like a benefactor.

69 Thank you. 70 Thank you. 71 Thank you.

72 you've changed my life

73 This is not true.

74 Not to the wheel. 75 Not to chance. 76 Not to the silent arithmetic 77 of probability.

78 But to the assigned face of chance 79 standing beside the mechanism 80 who did nothing 81 but witness.

82 They press their face 83 into his shoulder 84 like he chose them.

85 Like he gave.

86 Like the money 87 came from him.

88 This is the strangest part: 89 grief is mechanised, 90 luck is televised, 91 and gratitude 92 is misdirected.

93 A hug 94 where a question mark 95 should be.

96 A thank-you 97 where ethics 98 should be.

99 The audience claps 100 because someone else won. 101 They had a hobby. 102 A holiday planned. 103 A dream that fit neatly 104 inside a question.

105 Meanwhile, a children's ward 106 carries on breathing.

107 Meanwhile, a cancer support centre 108 counts quieter donations.

109 The wheel smiles 110 in glitter.

111 Michael smiles 112 because the rules are clean.

113 And they are. 114 No corruption. 115 No cheating. 116 Just a flawless machine 117 built to make suffering 118 compete with luck.

119 It keeps turning 120 past hospice windows, 121 past oncology chairs, 122 past ice-cream vans 123 parked at the edge 124 of grief.

125 It spins 126 and nothing in it 127 knows 128 why anyone 129 is really there.

— Lilith
