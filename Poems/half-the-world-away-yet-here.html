Half the World Away, Yet Here

1 I was eleven 2 when the calendar split 3 into before and after.

4 Year 6 ended mid-sentence— 5 no SATs, no leavers' day— 6 just empty desks, 7 forgotten jumpers, 8 and the smell of pencil shavings 9 trapped in the dark.

10 High school began 11 with masks and one-way arrows, 12 voices muffled, 13 friendships carried through glass and pixels. 14 We queued in taped-off lines, 15 sanitiser stinging our hands, 16 eyes doing the smiling for us.

17 I knew it was awful— 18 every news broadcast 19 a roll call of the gone. 20 I scrolled past names I didn't know, 21 but someone's favourite person was gone. 22 And sometimes, 23 it was a name the whole world knew— 24 Captain Sir Tom Moore, 25 Bilbo Baggins, 26 Chadwick Boseman, 27 the voices and faces 28 we thought would be there longer. 29 For a moment, 30 the grief was the same in every country.

31 I learned words 32 I shouldn't have needed so young: 33 lockdown, furlough, ventilator. 34 Whole cities stood still 35 while ambulances screamed 36 through their empty veins.

37 And yet— 38 in my own small world 39 there was warmth I'd never felt before. 40 Mum home in the evenings, 41 guiding us through maths and English, 42 teaching me the order of sharps and flats 43 in a key signature. 44 Dad cooking lunch, 45 setting up little science experiments— 46 vinegar fizzing in jam jars, 47 paper bridges buckling under coins. 48 Our cousins stayed for months, 49 the house becoming 50 one long classroom and one long sleepover— 51 learning, laughing, 52 growing closer than we'd ever been before.

53 The streets were quieter, 54 the air lighter— 55 like the planet itself 56 had taken a breath. 57 We'd clapped for strangers 58 we'd never meet, 59 shared bread still hot from the overn, 60 spoke the same anxious numbers.

61 The world was small enough 62 to fit inside one news story, 63 large enough 64 for the same moon 65 to watch over us all.

66 The worst thing and the gentlest thing 67 lived side by side: 68 grief on the news, 69 warmth in my kitchen. 70 The whole world 71 half the world away, 72 yet somehow 73 right here with me.

74 Events like this 75 rarely come along— 76 when the planet holds its breath 77 and every life bends 78 around the same story. 79 Other generations 80 had their own markers in time— 81 the moon landing, 82 the day the towers fell, 83 the first voice carried on radio waves, 84 the long nights of the Cold War. 85 Now we have ours. 86 Not in black-and-white photographs 87 or flickering newsreels, 88 but in our own lived hours— 89 the quiet streets, 90 the moonlit windows, 91 the feeling of the whole world 92 half the world away, 93 yet here 94 under that same patient moon.

95 And now we speak in new eras: 96 pre-Covid, post-Covid— 97 as if a virus 98 rewrote the seasons, 99 as if time itself paused and restarted.

100 Sometimes I look at the night sky 101 and wonder 102 if that same moon remembers us— 103 huddled indoors, 104 lit by blue screens, 105 trying to feel less alone 106 under its patient light.

107 — Lilith
