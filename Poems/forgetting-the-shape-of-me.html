Forgetting the Shape of Me

1 Feeling the feelings of others 2 for so long 3 that I am forgetting which 4 are mine,

5 standing in a narrow hallway, 6 air tasting of damp wood and old metal, 7 fingertips resting on a cold brass hinge 8 that does not open 9 but remembers opening, 10 pipes muttering behind the walls.

11 holding my hunger to the light, 12 tilting it, 13 watching sediment gather at the bottom of the glass, 14 not falling, 15 settling, 16 as if even motion has begun to doubt itself,

17 signals rising without language, 18 static crossing under my skin 19 like wires fitted to too many blueprints, 20 each drawn by a careful hand 21 that never learned the cost of care.

22 watching a choice split, 23 and split again, 24 not into spectacle 25 but into hardline fractures 26 creeping silently across a tiled floor, 27 geometry my body cannot pronounce,

28 as though awareness itself 29 were a kind of wrong gravity.

30 standing in front of a sentence 31 that should be harmless, 32 feeling it swell behind my teeth, 33 warm and misaligned, 34 like pressure building in a pipe 35 whose gauge knows too much 36 to be useful.

37 light loosening its grip on the wall, 38 dust rearranging itself mid-air, 39 my mind indexing evrey shift— 40 noting, 41 sorting, 42 over-calibrating each tremor until movement feels reckless, 43 measuring the breath of the room 44 like knowing too much to touch anything cleanly, 45 Aware of too many invisible ways to be wrong.

46 This should be simple.

47 My mouth tastes wrong.

48 weighing syllables in my palm 49 like bent instruments, 50 warped by a tuuning fork 51 that can hear too far, 52 all vibrations at once, 53 all slightly out of mercy, 54 no single tone safe enough 55 to trust.

56 Hovering between two switches — 57 truth / tenderness — 58 thumb shaking, 59 hand becoming a stuck valve, 60 open, closed, open, 61 leaking nothing 62 while pressure hums behind my ribs 63 like an unanswered theorem.

64 letting "I should just say it" 65 go stale inside my mouth, 66 spit thickening under my tongue, 67 rust blooming quietly 68 along the back of my throat, 69 while my heartbeat flickers between meanings 70 I understand too well 71 to follow blindly.

72 And doing nothing.

73 I am scared of making the wrong thing real.

74 Standing still 75 while the room grows louder, 76 not in sound 77 but in structure, 78 pipes expanding, 79 floorboards tightening, 80 lights sharpening along corners 81 like systems I can see through 82 but cannot unlearn.

83 I don't know what I feel.

84 cradling thin versions of myself 85 on narrow, leaning shelves, 86 labels written by hands 87 that thought they were kind, 88 edges tilted by arguments 89 I cannot disprove, 90 some tipping toward hunger, 91 some toward silence, 92 rebalancing weight 93 I never chose 94 but now cannot set down.

95 Lifting one. 96 Setting it back.

97 thirst arriving like a question 98 that has too many honest answers, 99 time pooling at my feet 100 like water circling a blocked drain, 101 still, 102 reflective, 103 unmoving 104 because it sees too much of the room.

105 pressing my tongue to the dark 106 of my own mouth, 107 pressing my hands storms 108 I do not own, 109 feelings other people's weather 110 strike my skin without warning — 111 too bright, 112 too loud, 113 too close, 114 as if empathy were a field 115 with no shelter.

116 stagnating in a bent length of pipe, 117 green film growing slowly inside, 118 water forgetting which direction 119 means relief.

120 While somewhere 121 under pavement, 122 beneath stone, 123 a current keeps moving 124 without permission, 125 without analysis, 126 without apology.

127 And still— 128 breathing, 129 standing over a hairline crack in the floorboards, 130 feeling the feelings of others 131 for so long 132 that I am forgetting which 133 are mine, 134 feeling pressure gather without language, 135 frameworks bending under bone, 136 until ever water, 137 quietly, 138 inevitably, 139 finds a seam.

— Lilith
