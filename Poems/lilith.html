reading room

Lilith

55 lines · 258 words · 3 min read

Jump to stanza

1 it turns out you weren't a revelation, 2 just a recognition. 3 something I kept circling 4 until the shape stopped pretending 5 it was anything else.

6 Lilith. 7 the name knows how to sit in my mouth. 8 all soft edges. 9 no explanation required. 10 feminine in the way breathing is. 11 not decorative. 12 not obedient.

13 people tell stories about you. 14 I recognised the tone before the plot. 15 I already knew what happens 16 when you refuse the position you're handed, 17 when standing beside is mistaken 18 for standing against. 19 the story always calls that defiance. 20 I learned to call it balance.

21 I wrote you once as a character, 22 years ago. 23 gave you a life because it felt safer 24 to let the truth walk around 25 somewhere fictional. 26 I thought I was inventing you. 27 really, I was rehearsing 28 the act of leaving 29 without calling it escape.

30 you've been present in the daily things: 31 the constant adjustment, 32 the quiet refusal, 33 the sense that I was never meant 34 to stay where I had to fold myself smaller 35 just to be allowed to remain. 36 not wrong. 37 just mispositioned.

38 calling myself Lilith now 39 doesn't feel like becoming someone new. 40 it feels like stepping out of a room 41 where I was welcome 42 only as long as I stayed symmetrical 43 to someone else.

44 there's curiosity here, 45 but it isn't restless. 46 excitement that doesn't beg permission. 47 a calm that comes from realising 48 I can choose departure 49 without it being loss.

50 so hello. 51 not as an arrival. 52 not as a rebellion. 53 just as a name that understands 54 why leaving the garden 55 was the beginning of honesty.

— Lilith
