The Light Changes Key

108 lines · 435 words · 5 min read

Jump to stanza

1 And the world wakes up. 2 And it looks at me. 3 And it doesn't like what it sees.

4 So it blinks. 5 So it names me wrong. 6 So it asks me to become 7 easier to explain.

8 But I am still breathing— 9 slow as tide, 10 slow as a word I haven't learned yet.

11 I keep my colour in my mouth. 12 I keep my name in my chest.

13 And maybe it won't love me. 14 And maybe that's okay.

15 I didn't come here to be wanted. 16 I came here 17 to stay.

18 Some mornings 19 that line goes quiet.

20 Some mornings the world rises 21 without me in it.

22 Light walks across the wall 23 like it doesn't need my permission.

24 The day speaks. 25 I say nothing. 26 This is one of those days.

27 The alarm sings once— 28 I let it finish.

29 The second time 30 it feels like accusation.

31 My phone fills with small demands: 32 be clear, be useful, be more.

33 The ceiling holds me 34 the way water holds a body 35 that has stopped kicking.

36 I think about the mirror 37 and how it keeps insisting. 38 How I say girl 39 and the room hears almost. 40 How being seen can feel 41 like being edited mid-sentence, 42 cut before the meaning arrives.

43 I think about the friend 44 who didn't fade— 45 six months ago, 46 a night of messages, 47 each word sharpened, 48 each silence louder than the last. 49 No warning. 50 Just a before 51 and an after 52 that still hasn't learned 53 how to touch.

54 I think about how the world loves lines— 55 straight ones, 56 productive ones, 57 lines that move forward without stopping. 58 As if my brain were a clock 59 that refuses to keep time 60 out of spite. 61 As if rest were theft. 62 As if difference were debt.

63 The day waits. 64 Still. 65 Unforgiving.

66 Productivity hums somewhere far away, 67 a song I know 68 but can't sing yet.

69 And for a moment 70 I don't want to stay. 71 Not loudly. 72 Not dangerously. 73 Just in the small way 74 where staying feels heavier 75 than sleep, 76 where even breathing feels like effort.

77 Then— 78 something small. 79 The light changes key. 80 Beautifully. 81 Just enough.

82 My chest rises 83 without asking 84 what I plan to do next. 85 Loss loosens its grip 86 and becomes space. 87 The friend I lost 88 taught me how deeply I can feel. 89 The futures I failed 90 taught me which ones 91 were never mine.

92 This body— 93 argued with, misread— 94 has still carried me 95 through every morning 96 that asked too much.

97 Nothing resolves. 98 Nothing redeems itself. 99 But I am still here. 100 Not as proof. 101 Not as promise. 102 Just as breath. 103 Just as weight.

104 I sit up. 105 The floor meets my feet. 106 The world wakes up— 107 and this time, 108 I go with it.

— Lilith
