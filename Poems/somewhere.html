reading room

Somewhere

37 lines · 170 words · 2 min read

1 I scroll through the old messages, 2 not to remember 3 but to feel if loss still glows.

4 Each line is untouched, 5 preserved like frost on glass. 6 You still speak to me there, 7 bright as if the sun never set on us.

8 The philosophers call it 9 the block universe, 10 everything happening at once, 11 nothing really gone. 12 I think that's what grief becomes: 13 a stillness that shines 14 where affection doesn't fade, 15 it just stops being received.

16 A-theory says the world keeps moving. 17 But these texts don't. 18 They stay, 19 beating softly inside a server. 20 Sometimes I envy them: 21 their refusal to decay, 22 their peace with permanence.

23 C-theory tells me even motion 24 is an illusion. 25 Moments are just beads on a string, 26 no before, no after, 27 only position. 28 And I am somewhere 29 between the bead where you laughed 30 and the bead where you left.

31 If I could unlearn grammar, 32 I'd forget how to say once. 33 I'd forget how to say after. 34 I'd say somewhere 35 and mean: 36 you are still typing 37 and I am still waiting.

— Lilith
