reading room

FIELD GUIDE

Jump to stanza

1 my body's a room with every light on. 2 a radio tuuned to all stations at once. 3 i'm trying to sit still 4 but my thoughts spin chairs.

5 in year seven, i learned 6 how to pretend i was listening. 7 nod at the right time, 8 underline the date, 9 draw galaxies on the margins.

10 i feel my mother's worry in my jaw, 11 my father's restlessness in my knees. 12 some days i dance without music. 13 some days i forget to eat.

14 i once told a teacher: 15 i couldn't remember what she just said. 16 she frowned like i'd confessed to sin. 17 i swallowed my "sorry" 18 and it rattled for hours.

19 sometimes, my breath forgets itself.

20 sometimes, my limbs wander off mid-sentence.

21 i lose whole hours 22 and still feel late.

23 i do not how to say 24 "i'm trying" 25 without hearing it as an excuse. 26 but i try.

27 today i lie deep down in the grass 28 and allow my thoughts to go wherever they please. 29 the sky doesn't scold me 30 for being everything at once.

— Lilith
