Eulogy for the Melting Giant

1 I was born before your calendars, 2 before the word "beginning" found its breath. 3 Snow upon snow, 4 layered like sleep over centuries— 5 I remembered silence long after you forgot it.

6 I carved valleys with the patience of gods. 7 Spoke only in pressure. 8 Taught rivers how to walk. 9 Held time in my blue-lit belly, 10 while stars blinked 11 through the slow dance of ages.

12 You named me "resource." 13 "Scenic view." 14 You framed my dying breath in brochures.

15 I heard the engines first— 16 a distant growl beneath the clouds. 17 Then came your warmth, 18 like a lie that lingers. 19 You fed the air to flames 20 and call it progress.

21 You brought smoke to my snow. 22 Heat in my marrow. 23 I cracked, I wept, I shrank— 24 and still 25 you looked away... 26 holding your phones 27 like shields against grief.

28 I do not die easily. 29 I am not a puddle. 30 I am memory liquefied. 31 I am centuries collapsing 32 into soundless flood.

33 Still— 34 beneath my grief, 35 I carry awe.

36 For the fox that danced across my spine. 37 For the child who touched me with reverence. 38 For the whisper of wind 39 still singing lullabies to the peaks I once kissed.

40 And when I am gone, 41 you may not remember my name, 42 but you will remember 43 what the world was like 44 when I still held it cold and clean.

45 If this is a eulogy, 46 then let it also be a warning— 47 you do not survive 48 by forgetting your giants, 49 nor do they forgive 50 being forgotten.

— Lilith
