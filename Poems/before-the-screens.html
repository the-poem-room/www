Before the Screens

I still recall what silence used to say—
before the red lights glowed across our screens.
The stars were clearer when we looke away.

We lived like clocks that ticked but lost their way,
forgetting how the golden hours wane.
I still recall what silence used to say.

A stream would murmur softly as it lay
across the stones, untouched by bright machines.
The stars were clearer when we looked away.

We traded sky for signal, earth for steel,
in every pixelated frame of days gone by.
I still recall what silence used to say—

The future hums, but never wants to stay.
It flickers past through code and fading loops.
The stars were clearer when we looked away.

And thoughe world will fade to screen-washed grey,
I hold the hush that hummed in softer scenes.
I still recall what silence used to say—
the stars burned clearer when we looked away.

— Lilith
