Seventeen Haikus for Iceland

Jump to stanza

1 1 2 Snowfield without path — 3 the wind invents its own script 4 across the silence.

5 2 6 Reykjavík breathes light. 7 Smoke, laughter, and winter coats 8 soften every street.

9 3 10 Midnight fireworks — 11 the sky blooms in every hue. 12 Even cold applauds.

13 4 14 Frozen waterfall 15 pauses mid-sentence of stone, 16 held by breathless air.

17 5 18 Black sand under frost — 19 each wave forgets where to go, 20 lost beneath pale skies.

21 6 22 Hot srings blur the cold. 23 Steam drifts like a memory 24 not quite forgiven.

25 7 26 A shark on my plate. 27 The taste: stubborn-sea-buried — 28 I chew through the past.

29 8 30 Geyser's sudden roar — 31 the still world flinches, then sighes, 32 letting go of heat.

33 9 34 Cracks in the glacier 35 shine with some ancient blue light. 36 Ice remembers us.

37 10 38 A horse in the snow 39 blinks, then turns — as if it knows 40 this moment won't last.

41 11 42 Selfoss: one quiet road, 43 each lamp a gentle heartbeat 44 in the drifting dusk.

45 12 46 Volcanic black stone 47 dressed in moon-reflecting frost — 48 fire disguised as calm.

49 13 50 A tune in the wind — 51 is it Björk or just the land 52 humming to itself?

53 14 54 The sun barely wakes, 55 lingers at the edge of things — 56 a ghost of a flame.

57 15 58 Snow refills my steps 59 before I've moved on from them. 60 Even time retreats.

61 16 62 Silence so complete, 63 I almost hear the mountain 64 shift beneath the snow.

65 17 66 I pause in the white — 67 no, path, no sound, only breath. 68 I am what remains.

— Lilith
