reading room

How To

Jump to stanza

1 1. Symptoms: 2     You spot two men holding hands. 3     Your heart attempts a drum solo. 4     A 90s sitcom laugh track boots up in your skull.

5 2. Diagnosis: 6     Not an emergency. 7     Just attraction existing in the open air, like oxygen or pigeons.

8 3. Immediate care: 9     Breathe. 10     If breathing fails, try blinking. 11     If blinking fails, try minding your own business. 12     (Clinical trials show a 100% success rate.)

13 4. Common myths: 14     — Queerness is contagious? 15     Nope. You can stand near a rainbow and still like whoever you like. 16     — Compliments cause conversions? 17     Nope. “Nice jacket” is a sentence, not a spell.

18 5. De-escalation script: 19     (beginner) “Hi.” 20     (intermediate) “Cool shoes.” 21     (advanced) “Have a great day.” 22     If you’ve made it this far, congratulations: you survived courtesy.

23 6. Environmental notes: 24     Two women kissing won’t lower your battery life. 25     A non-binary person entering a room won’t tilt the earth’s axis. 26     Someone flirting politely won’t steal your masculinity; 27     you’ll find that under your bed— 28     keeping company with your don’t-ask sock.

29 7. When to call for help: 30     If you hear sirens, they’re for an actual thing 31     like a cat on a roof or a kettle left on; 32     not for you witnessing joy in public.

33 8. Long-term treatment: 34     Update the software between your ears. 35     Uninstall “gay panic (beta).” 36     Install “other people are people (stable release).”

37 9. Prognosis: 38     Excellent. Side effects include 39     less fear, more friends, 40     and the shocking realisation 41     that love, in all its outfits, isn’t out to get you— 42     it’s just out.

— Lilith
