Between Hours

1 Time has never been a strict thing, 2 not really.

3 In Iceland, the sun forgets 4 its bedtime — 5 morning lingers, 6 night take its time 7 deciding if it wants to arrive at all. 8 Hours stretch like curious arms, 9 reaching toward winter fireworks, 10 daylight stitched with quiet crackles, 11 a small daylight revolt, 12 the horizon unsure 13 what hour it holds.

14 Here at home 15 curtains closed, 16 the clock keeps its habits 17 but I don't always listen. 18 Pages fill, melodies spill, 19 one idea nudging the next, 20 thoughts tugging loose, 21 seconds loosening, 22 lost and briefly found, 23 a quiet countdown 24 dissolving into hush.

25 Time blurs 26 when I stop measuring it, 27 when the world outside 28 is simply allowed 29 to be wherever it is, 30 and I let myself be 31 right here, 32 in the honest hush 33 between heartbeats, 34 where light and thought 35 share the same soft space.

36 There's a kind of beauty 37 in forgetting the rules 38 we invented anyway: 39 that morning must be bright, 40 that evening must be dark, 41 that minutes must march 42 instead of dance — 43 instead of chance, 44 instead of slipping softly 45 out of step.

46 Maybe the most honest moments 47 are the ones that refuse 48 to find inside a calendar square, 49 the ones where a day 50 feels boundless, 51 where I float, 52 unbothered by ticking, 53 simply existing 54 in the soft stretch 55 between any beginning 56 and any ending, 57 the air still bending light, 58 whispering 59 that time is real 60 when we forget to care, 61 when we drift 62 between hours.

— Lilith
