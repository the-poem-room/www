Children See More

1 I once saw a crack 2 in the pavement 3 and thought maybe 4 something was trying to escape— 5 a root, 6 a ripple, 7 a hidden word.

8 Mum said, 9 "It's just the frost," 10 but I knew 11 some things split 12 because they want to grow.

13 I saw a girl 14 offer her seat on the bus— 15 no words, 16 just a small move 17 that felt like a quiet promise 18 to someone who needed it more. 19 She didn't smile. 20 She didn't know 21 it was the kindest thing 22 I'd seen all day. 23 Maybe she still doesn't.

24 Once, a puddle 25 held a piece of sky. 26 I told them— 27 the clouds were floating in it— 28 but they laughed 29 and called me dreamy. 30 The next day, 31 the puddle was gone. 32 The sky stayed.

33 I watched a shadow 34 climb the wall 35 as if it had somewhere to be. 36 I think time moves like that— 37 quiet, 38 slow, 39 and only noticed 40 when you stop playing.

41 They say 42 children imagine things. 43 But maybe we just 44 see the parts 45 that adults forget— 46 the shimmer 47 on the edge of normal, 48 the meaning 49 tucked behind small things.

50 We don't grow out of it. 51 We grow around it, 52 like trees bending 53 to fences 54 but never forgetting 55 the sky.

56 And one day, 57 when it's quiet, 58 we'll remember 59 what it felt like 60 to see 61 without asking why.

62 Not more, exactly. 63 Just 64 differently. 65 Just 66 deeper.

— Lilith
