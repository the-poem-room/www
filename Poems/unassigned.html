reading room

Unassigned

Jump to stanza

1 There's so much love 2 inside me— 3 but who is it for?

4 It doesn't arrive as tenderness. 5 It arrives as pressure— 6 a sentence forming faster than etiquette 7 can intervene, 8 my mouth already saying I love you 9 before my mind has checked 10 whether that's allowed.

11 Sometimes it is a person. 12 A laugh on a Discord call, 13 a pause where no one rushes to fill the silence, 14 a kindness that lands with no intention 15 of being important. 16 The phrase escapes me 17 like a reflex test I didn't consent to.

18 Sometimes it is not a person at all. 19 Just the sensation— 20 static without a source, 21 a warmth that isnt gentle, 22 a memory misfiring 23 and hitting someone who no longer exists 24 except as atmosphere.

25 What am I meant to do 26 with this surplus?

27 I have tried 28 and failed 29 to swallow it.

30 Love moves through me 31 like a system doing its job too well, 32 converting laughter into devotion, 33 gratitude into something 34 people flinch back from, 35 leaving me on read 36 without explanation, 37 calling it boundaries. 38 I try smaller words— 39 thank you, I appreciate you— 40 but they come out underpowered, 41 spoken at half the voltage 42 my body is running on.

43 I don't mean permanence. 44 I don't mean ownership. 45 I don't mean romance, or sex, 46 or the slow architecture of a future.

47 I mean: 48 this reached me— 49 and I didn't look away.

50 They say love should be directed, 51 aimed, 52 spent carefully. 53 Kept in labelled containers, 54 away from heat. 55 No one tells you what happens 56 when it's ambient. 57 When it leaks into friendships, 58 ruins the mood, 59 makes sincerity feel suspicious.

60 I have lost people this way. 61 Not dramatically. 62 Just quietly— 63 the way you lose objects 64 you were holding too tightly 65 without realising.

66 Sometimes I think 67 this love is not a message at all 68 but a condition— 69 not for anyone, 70 not about anyone, 71 just the way my nervous system 72 reacts to meaning 73 before it asks permission.

74 A girl frinding herself 75 and realising he volume knob 76 was never installed.

77 If this love has nowhere to go, 78 maybe that's the point. 79 Maybe it doesn't need a subject 80 to be real.

81 There's so much love 82 inside me— 83 unassigned, 84 relentless, 85 uncontained, 86 awake.

87 If it doesn't belong to you, 88 or to anyone, 89 maybe that doesn't make it wasted.

90 Maybe it belongs 91 to attention. 92 To survival.

93 To the simple, dangerous fact 94 that I am here, 95 and the world 96 keeps reaching me— 97 and I keep responding

— Lilith
