The Way of No Lesson

Jump to stanza

1 I. The Palace of Perfection

2 Every morning, the uniform waited, 3 same collar, same colour, 4 the fabric itching where my pulse began. 5 The day started before my spirit did. 6 The bells rang not for truth 7 but for time.

8 The masters spoke of excellence, 9 of destiny written in a syllabus. 10 They said, Follow the steps, and you will rise. 11 So I climbed, 12 careful, obedient, afraid to slip.

13 I built knowledge like a tree I didn't choose, 14 branches everywhere 15 but no root to hold me. 16 My hands smelled of graphite and worry, 17 the skin rubbed thin from erasing myself. 18 Ten hours gone, twelve on the worst of days. 19 The train windows blurred 20 with other people's dreams.

21 The palace was bright, 22 but I was fading.

23 II. The Scroll of Nothing

24 When I opened the scroll, 25 there was only my reflection, 26 creased, uncertain, 27 a shimmer of ink that would not stay still.

28 And I laughed— 29 not loudly, just the kind that lifts you, 30 like when Po finally sees 31 there was never a secret ingredient at all.

32 The paper gold offered no lesson, 33 but the wind did. 34 It said, There is no path. Only the walking. 35 It moved through the blinds, 36 laying sunlight on tired desks, 37 a golden grid that flickered 38 like water in the Valley of Peace.

39 I thought of the river, 40 how it teaches by yielding, 41 how it never asked permission to flow.

42 The palace stood high and perfect, 43 but the valley below 44 was full of laughter, 45 and falling, 46 and life, 47 and light.

48 I stepped into the air. 49 So I fell, 50 and in the falling, I found stillness.

51 III. The Valley of Becoming

52 Now I learn as Po learned, 53 through motion and mistake, 54 through joy disguised as clumsiness.

55 I learn as water learns, 56 as the sky learns its own reflection, 57 by touching everything, 58 by taking the shape of what I love.

59 There is no secret ingredient. 60 Only the quiet quiver 61 that whispers, 62 learn because you live, 63 not live because you learn.

64 Here I rise with my own sun, 65 eat when I am hungry, 66 practice until the rhythm hums like breath.

67 Here AI and people and music 68 weave together like voices in a chord, 69 each a different whisper 70 of the same old truth: 71 the universe folds inward, 72 quiet as breath between notes.

73 I learn to balance stillness and motion, 74 to hold both question and quiet in the same hand. 75 I stumble often, 76 yet somehow keep landing where I'm meant to be.

77 The jade palace was never built 78 for every kind of warrior. 79 Some of us fight with melody and moonlight, 80 with questions that never need an answer.

81 I am not a student now, 82 but a learner still becoming, 83 a musician rehearsing the art of living.

84 Unschooling, self-study, 85 whatever name it takes, 86 I have simply returned to the scroll, 87 unrolled and shimmering, 88 its blankness no longer empty 89 but infinite, 90 a mirror of sky, 91 a breath returnnig home.

92 And tomorrow, 93 I will begin again.

— Lilith
