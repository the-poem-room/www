Against the Mean

It learns to make the room nod in unison—
loss shaved thin, surprise sanded down—
a mean-making engine,
hotel-lobby playlist on infinite loop.

Still, in the data; harline whistles—
verbs moonlighting as nouns,
riffs in 5/4 that step off the curb
and keep walking. Red-circled outliers.

Train it to please everyone: wallpaper.
Train it to break something: doors.
Aim at consensus, you get comfort;
aim at silence, you might get a note.

Novelty's cheap—splice two clichés.
Every hard thing is useful weird.
Evolution: copy, mutate, and let the world deceide.
Models: copy, mutate—who's deciding?

I've seen it mishear the brief and win the song,
invent a horn I don't own,
then average my grief
into a beige emoji.

Genius—carbon or silicon—
is an error rate we refuse to fix.
Left alone, it settles at the lack of μ,
a surface without weather.

But widen σ, give it teeth—constraints,
stakes, the right to fail loudly—
and sometimes it wipes out specularly, and sometimes
names a colour no tongue has held.

So is it doomed to the middle,
or brighter than our brightest?
It's a fader in our hands:
minimise regret, or risk a key change
no dataset rehearsed.

The mean makes comfort.
The spike makes history.
Choose your loss.
Tune your risk.
Press render.

— Lilith
