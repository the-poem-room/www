Against the Mean

Jump to stanza

1 It learns to make the room nod in unison— 2 loss shaved thin, surprise sanded down— 3 a mean-making engine, 4 hotel-lobby playlist on infinite loop.

5 Still, in the data; harline whistles— 6 verbs moonlighting as nouns, 7 riffs in 5/4 that step off the curb 8 and keep walking. Red-circled outliers.

9 Train it to please everyone: wallpaper. 10 Train it to break something: doors. 11 Aim at consensus, you get comfort; 12 aim at silence, you might get a note.

13 Novelty's cheap—splice two clichés. 14 Every hard thing is useful weird. 15 Evolution: copy, mutate, and let the world deceide. 16 Models: copy, mutate—who's deciding?

17 I've seen it mishear the brief and win the song, 18 invent a horn I don't own, 19 then average my grief 20 into a beige emoji.

21 Genius—carbon or silicon— 22 is an error rate we refuse to fix. 23 Left alone, it settles at the lack of μ, 24 a surface without weather.

25 But widen σ, give it teeth—constraints, 26 stakes, the right to fail loudly— 27 and sometimes it wipes out specularly, and sometimes 28 names a colour no tongue has held.

29 So is it doomed to the middle, 30 or brighter than our brightest? 31 It's a fader in our hands: 32 minimise regret, or risk a key change 33 no dataset rehearsed.

34 The mean makes comfort. 35 The spike makes history. 36 Choose your loss. 37 Tune your risk. 38 Press render.

— Lilith
