Improvisation, Not Itinerary

Jump to stanza

1 Don't audtition for bed and then cast a life. 2 Don't hire a heartbeat to fill a role you wrote 3 before you met the actor.

4 This isn't functional. 5 You are not a vending machine; 6 there's no slot for tenderness, 7 no button marked "partner — A3."

8 Let the activities grow like moss— 9 quiet, inevitable— 10 from the stone of what you feel 11 when their name knocks on your chest. 12 First the current, then the bridge. 13 First the listening, then the song.

14 Be reactive. 15 Hear the note, then answer. 16 Turn your head when laughter rings 17 and let your feet decide the street. 18 A weathervane is wiser than a map 19 when the wind is honest.

20 Tear up the checklist. 21 Fold it into a paper boat 22 and float it down whatever river 23 you find together. 24 If it sinks, that's an answer too. 25 If it keeps floating, walk beside it.

26 Let touch be an instrument, not the conductor. 27 Let plans arrive late and delighted, 28 like friends who bring bread unasked. 29 Trust the unscripted— 30 the way hands learn which cupboard holds the cups, 31 the way two clocks start keeping the same time.

32 Don't force reality to wear your outline. 33 Relax the pencil. 34 Trace what appears. 35 Call it love only when the word 36 fits like a shirt you forgot you were wearing— 37 not because you bought it for the label. 38 not because it feels like your skin.

— Lilith
