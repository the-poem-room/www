A Practical Guide to My Pronouns

75 lines · 394 words · 4 min read

1 Shall they say he? 2 Well, yes, they shall, and I'll twitch like a smoke alarm 3 catching whiffs of burnt toast, 4 but not burst into flames.

5 Shall they say she? 6 That one feels like wearing shoes two sizes off: 7 tight in the toes, loose in the heel, 8 clumsy to walk in, but walkable.

9 Shall they say they? 10 Fine, though I can hear the grammar polic 11 loading their rifles in the distance.

12 Shall they instead 13 recite the full inventory: citizen, taxpayer, 14 carbon-based biped, 15 entity formerly known as male, 16 until language itself is 17 exhausted.

18 It's l ike asking for bread 19 and being offered gluten-free, yeast-free, 20 air-fried, paleo-adjacent flact circles of despair— 21 when all I wanted was bread.

22 And what of my girlfriend? 23 Must she introduce me as boyfriend, partner, 24 "significant other," 25 or *the individual currently under contract 26 to hold her hand at parties?* 27 For heavan's sake, just let us be!

28 And no— 29 I do not wander into boy's changing rooms, 30 pretending I belong there. 31 Some lines I won't cross. 32 Some spaces are too heavy with other people's certainty, 33 too sharp with stares 34 to make my small compromises worth it.

35 Avoidance is its own form of survival.

36 Because here is the truth: 37 it isn't the words themselves, 38 sharp little stones in my boot, 39 that trouble me most. 40 It's the smile that says I'm humouring you, 41 the silence that says you're not real, 42 the pause that says why can't you just pick one?

43 Language is a tool, 44 sometimes a cage, 45 sometimes a joke stretched too long, 46 but always a mirror polished 47 by whoever holds it. 48 I could demand every reflection 49 match me exactly— 50 but then I'd become the archivist of labels, 51 dusting shelves no one reads 52 while the world keeps moving.

53 So call me he, 54 and I'll wince, but keep walking. 55 Call me she, 56 and I'll sigh, but not collapse. 57 Call me they, 58 and I'll nod at your effort, 59 though English would rather 60 split its own infinitives 61 than loosen its grip on gender.

62 Because I am not here 63 to drill obedience into your tongue. 64 I am here to live, 65 to speak, 66 to matter. 67 And if I must choose 68 between a life where words are pristine 69 and a life where words are messy but mine, 70 I'll take the twinge, 71 the pinch, 72 the occasional semantic glitch.

73 Because I know the surface can bruise me, 74 but the depths— 75 the depths decide everything.

— Lilith
