about me

Bio

My name is Lilith Foxcroft. I'm a poet and musician from Stoke-on-Trent. I compose, perform, and spend a probably unreasonable amount of time thinking about art and sound.

In 2025, I won the Wells Young Poets Competition for a poem originally titled A Field Guide to Focus, later renamed FIELD GUIDE. The poem was published under my deadname, Ben.

Most of what there is to know about me can probably be found somewhere in my poetry, but here are a few recurring little obsessions, interests, and facts I tend to mention when introducing myself, as well as a preview of my competition-winning poem.

Recurring little obsessions

  • I’ve been vegan since July 23rd, 2025, mostly for ethical reasons.
  • I love quiet walks, philosophy, and poetry that feels emotionally honest.
  • Favourite poets include Emily Dickinson, Sylvia Plath, Ocean Vuong, Mary Oliver, Rainer Maria Rilke, T. S. Eliot, Emily Brontë, Walt Whitman, Anne Carson, and Richard Siken.
  • I’m home-educated.
  • I always type my poems out in full, and never copy and paste them between apps.
  • I occasionally upload videos to a YouTube channel called Lilith (@LilithHive).
  • I love philosophy, especially existentialism, ethics, and questions surrounding identity and consciousness. Some of my favourite philosophers include Arthur Schopenhauer, Derek Parfit, Albert Camus, A. J. Ayer, Peter Singer, Bertrand Russell, and Ludwig Wittgenstein.

competion-winning poem

FIELD GUIDE

my body's a room with every light on.
a radio tuuned to all stations at once.
i'm trying to sit still
but my thoughts spin chairs.

in year seven, i learned
how to pretend i was listening.
nod at the right time,
underline the date,
draw galaxies on the margins.

i feel my mother's worry in my jaw,
my father's restlessness in my knees.
some days i dance without music.
some days i forget to eat.

i once told a teacher:
i couldn't remember what she just said.
she frowned like i'd confessed to sin.
i swallowed my "sorry"
and it rattled for hours.

sometimes, my breath forgets itself.

sometimes, my limbs wander off mid-sentence.

i lose whole hours
and still feel late.

i do not how to say
"i'm trying"
without hearing it as an excuse.
but i try.

today i lie deep down in the grass
and allow my thoughts to go wherever they please.
the sky doesn't scold me
for being everything at once.

— Lilith

A lot of my work explores memory, uncertainty, identity, tenderness, philosophy, ethics, overstimulation, and the strange experience of trying to understand yourself in a world that rarely slows down.

Outside of writing, I spend much of my time making music, overthinking things, and caring deeply about people, animals, honesty, and art that feels sincere.

More than anything, I hope this little room of poems gives you something meaningful to return to.
